2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Triple sulfa combinations work through competitive inhibition of bacterial folate synthesis. Each component (sulfadiazine, sulfamerazine, sulfamethazine) inhibits dihydropteroate synthase by mimicking para-aminobenzoic acid (PABA). This prevents bacterial DNA synthesis and cell division, producing bacteriostatic effects. The combination provides broader spectrum coverage and may reduce resistance development compared to single-agent therapy.

Clinical Utility

Historically used for urinary tract infections, respiratory tract infections, and other bacterial conditions. Modern clinical utility is limited due to availability of more effective antibiotics with better safety profiles. Primary concerns include sulfonamide hypersensitivity reactions, blood dyscrasias, and crystalluria. Generally considered for temporary use during acute bacterial infections rather than long-term therapy.
