## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action
Timolol functions as a non-selective beta-adrenergic receptor antagonist. In ophthalmic applications, it reduces intraocular pressure by decreasing aqueous humor production through blockade of Œ≤-adrenergic receptors in the ciliary muscle. This mechanism works within the eye's natural pressure regulation system, modulating the response to endogenous catecholamines that normally stimulate aqueous humor production.

### Clinical Utility
Primary therapeutic applications include treatment of elevated intraocular pressure in patients with ocular hypertension or open-angle glaucoma. Timolol ophthalmic solution is typically used twice daily and has demonstrated efficacy in reducing intraocular pressure by 20-30%. The medication has a well-established safety profile for topical ophthalmic use, with systemic absorption being minimal compared to oral administration. It serves as a first-line treatment option that can delay or prevent the need for more invasive glaucoma surgeries.
