# Assessment of DEFACTINIB

## 1. NATURAL DERIVATION ASSESSMENT

### Source Investigation
Defactinib (VS-6063) is a synthetic small molecule drug with no direct natural source identified.

### Structural Analysis

  • Synthetic compound with a pyridine core structure
  • No direct structural analogs found in nature
  • Functions as a focal adhesion kinase (FAK) inhibitor

### Biological Mechanism Evaluation

  • Targets naturally occurring FAK signaling pathway
  • Interacts with cellular adhesion mechanisms
  • Affects cellular migration and survival pathways

### Natural System Integration

  • Targets endogenous FAK enzyme
  • Affects natural cell survival mechanisms
  • Influences cellular adhesion processes
  • Works within existing cellular signaling networks

## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action

  • Selective inhibitor of focal adhesion kinase (FAK)
  • Blocks phosphorylation of FAK at Tyr397
  • Disrupts cancer cell survival signaling
  • Affects cellular adhesion and migration

### Clinical Utility

  • Investigated for treatment of various cancers
  • Particular focus on mesothelioma
  • Under investigation for solid tumors
  • Generally administered orally

### Integration Potential

  • Limited integration with natural therapies
  • Primarily used in conventional oncology
  • Requires specialist oversight
  • Complex monitoring requirements
