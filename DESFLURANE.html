2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Desflurane functions as a general anesthetic through multiple mechanisms including enhancement of inhibitory neurotransmission (primarily through GABA-A and glycine receptors) and depression of excitatory neurotransmission. It also affects voltage-gated ion channels and may interact with lipid membranes. The result is dose-dependent central nervous system depression leading to unconsciousness, amnesia, and immobility.

Clinical Utility

Desflurane is used exclusively for maintenance of general anesthesia during surgical procedures. It offers rapid onset and emergence due to its low blood-gas solubility coefficient (0.42). It provides excellent hemodynamic stability and is particularly useful in ambulatory surgery due to its rapid recovery profile. The medication is administered only by trained anesthesia providers in controlled medical environments. It is strictly for temporary use during procedures.

3. REGULATORY AND PRECEDENT CONTEXT

Current Status

Desflurane is FDA-approved as a prescription medication (NDA 19-537, first approved 1992) classified as a general anesthetic. It is a controlled medical device requiring specialized vaporizers for administration. It is not included in the WHO Essential Medicines List, as the WHO list does not specify particular volatile anesthetics but rather includes "general anesthetics" as a class.
