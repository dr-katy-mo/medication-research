Mechanism of Action

Prasugrel is a third-generation thienopyridine that irreversibly inhibits the P2Y12 ADP receptor on platelets. Following oral administration, it undergoes rapid hydrolysis by esterases and subsequent oxidation by cytochrome P450 enzymes to form an active thiol metabolite. This metabolite forms a disulfide bridge with cysteine residues on the P2Y12 receptor, permanently blocking ADP binding and preventing conformational changes required for platelet activation and aggregation.
