Mechanism of Action

Fluorescein acts as a fluorescent diagnostic agent through its unique photophysical properties. When excited by blue light (485nm wavelength), it emits bright green fluorescence (520nm). In ophthalmology, it reveals corneal epithelial defects by pooling in areas where the protective epithelial layer is compromised. In angiography, it follows blood flow patterns, allowing visualization of retinal and choroidal circulation. The compound does not alter cellular function but rather serves as an inert tracer that highlights anatomical structures and physiological processes.
