Methotrexate is structurally very similar to folic acid, differing by the addition of an amino group and a methyl group. Mechanism of Action

Methotrexate functions as a folate antimetabolite, competitively inhibiting dihydrofolate reductase and other enzymes involved in folate metabolism. This results in decreased synthesis of thymidine and purines, ultimately reducing DNA synthesis and cellular replication. The mechanism directly interfaces with natural cellular metabolic pathways, specifically targeting the folate cycle that is essential for normal cellular function.
