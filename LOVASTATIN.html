2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Lovastatin functions as a competitive inhibitor of HMG-CoA reductase by mimicking the natural substrate HMG-CoA. After oral administration, the inactive lactone form is hydrolyzed to the active hydroxy acid form, which binds to the enzyme's active site. This reduces the conversion of HMG-CoA to mevalonate, the rate-limiting step in cholesterol synthesis. The mechanism preserves the body's natural feedback regulation while reducing excessive cholesterol production, allowing LDL receptors to upregulate naturally in response to decreased intracellular cholesterol.
