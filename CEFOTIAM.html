Mechanism of Action

Cefotiam exerts its antimicrobial effect by binding to penicillin-binding proteins located in the bacterial cell wall, inhibiting the final transpeptidation step of peptidoglycan synthesis. This results in cell wall weakening, osmotic instability, and bacterial cell lysis. The mechanism is highly selective for bacterial cells due to the absence of peptidoglycan in mammalian cells. The bactericidal action occurs rapidly and is concentration-dependent within therapeutic ranges.
