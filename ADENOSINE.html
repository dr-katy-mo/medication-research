2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Adenosine functions by binding to adenosine A1 receptors in the atrioventricular (AV) node, causing transient AV block through activation of potassium channels and inhibition of calcium channels. This mechanism mimics the natural role of endogenous adenosine in cardiac conduction regulation. The medication has an extremely short half-life (less than 10 seconds) due to rapid uptake by erythrocytes and endothelial cells through the same natural adenosine transport systems that handle endogenous adenosine. This rapid metabolism occurs through the same enzymatic pathways (adenosine deaminase and adenosine kinase) that process naturally occurring adenosine.

Clinical Utility

Adenosine is primarily used for acute termination of paroxysmal supraventricular tachycardia (PSVT) involving the AV node. It is considered first-line therapy for hemodynamically stable PSVT and is included in advanced cardiac life support protocols. The medication provides rapid therapeutic response (typically within 30 seconds) and has a favorable safety profile due to its extremely short duration of action. It is used for temporary therapeutic intervention rather than long-term treatment, allowing natural cardiac rhythm to be rapidly restored without ongoing pharmacological interference.
