Mechanism of Action

Semaglutide functions as a GLP-1 receptor agonist, mimicking the action of endogenous GLP-1 hormone. Upon binding to GLP-1 receptors, it activates adenylyl cyclase, increasing intracellular cAMP levels. This leads to glucose-dependent insulin secretion from pancreatic beta-cells, suppression of inappropriate glucagon secretion from alpha-cells, slowing of gastric emptying to moderate nutrient absorption, and activation of satiety centers in the hypothalamus. These mechanisms directly parallel natural postprandial (post-meal) physiological responses.

Current Status

Semaglutide is FDA-approved under multiple brand names: Ozempic (2017) for type 2 diabetes, Wegovy (2021) for chronic weight management, and Rybelsus (2019) as an oral formulation for diabetes.
