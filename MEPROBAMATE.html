Meprobamate functions as a central nervous system depressant by enhancing GABA-A receptor-mediated chloride influx, leading to neuronal hyperpolarization and reduced excitability. Clinical Utility

Meprobamate was historically used as an anxiolytic and muscle relaxant but has largely been replaced by benzodiazepines due to safety concerns.
