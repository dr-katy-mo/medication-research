2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Dapsone functions through two primary mechanisms: antimicrobial activity via competitive inhibition of PABA in bacterial folate synthesis, and anti-inflammatory activity through inhibition of neutrophil function and complement system modulation. The antimicrobial effect disrupts bacterial DNA synthesis by preventing folate production, while the anti-inflammatory effects reduce tissue damage from excessive immune responses.

Clinical Utility

Primary therapeutic applications include treatment of leprosy (Hansen's disease), Pneumocystis jirovecii pneumonia prophylaxis, dermatitis herpetiformis, and various autoimmune bullous skin conditions. Dapsone is particularly valuable for long-term management of chronic inflammatory skin conditions and as part of multidrug therapy for mycobacterial infections. The medication has a generally favorable safety profile but requires monitoring for methemoglobinemia and hemolytic anemia, especially in patients with G6PD deficiency.
