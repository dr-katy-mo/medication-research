2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Clomiphene functions as a selective estrogen receptor modulator, exhibiting both estrogenic and anti-estrogenic properties depending on the target tissue. In the hypothalamus and pituitary, it acts as an estrogen antagonist, blocking estrogen receptors and preventing negative feedback inhibition. This results in increased secretion of GnRH from the hypothalamus and subsequent elevation of FSH and LH from the pituitary, stimulating ovarian follicular development and ovulation in women, and testosterone production in men.

Clinical Utility

The primary therapeutic application is ovulation induction in women with anovulatory infertility, particularly those with polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS). It is also used off-label for male hypogonadism to stimulate endogenous testosterone production. The medication has a well-established safety profile when used for appropriate durations (typically 3-6 cycles). It offers a less invasive alternative to injectable gonadotropins for fertility treatment and allows for the restoration of natural hormonal cycling rather than hormone replacement.
