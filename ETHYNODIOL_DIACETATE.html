# Naturopathic Formulary Medication Evaluation Protocol
For ETHYNODIOL DIACETATE:

## 1. NATURAL DERIVATION ASSESSMENT

### Source Investigation

  • Not naturally occurring
  • Synthetic progestin derived from 19-nortestosterone
  • No traditional medicine use documented
  • Synthetic production methods only

### Structural Analysis

  • Structurally related to endogenous progesterone
  • Contains steroid nucleus common to natural hormones
  • Metabolizes to norethindrone
  • Shows structural similarity to other natural progestogens

### Biological Mechanism Evaluation

  • Interacts with progesterone receptors
  • Affects hypothalamic-pituitary-ovarian axis
  • Mimics natural progesterone effects
  • Integrates with endogenous hormonal systems

### Natural System Integration

  • Targets natural progesterone receptors
  • Modifies natural hormonal feedback loops
  • Works within existing reproductive physiology
  • Affects natural ovulation processes

## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action

  • Inhibits ovulation via hypothalamic feedback
  • Thickens cervical mucus
  • Changes endometrial environment
  • Suppresses follicular development

### Clinical Utility

  • Oral contraception (primary use)
  • Used in combination with ethinyl estradiol
  • Generally well-tolerated
  • Temporary intervention for birth control

### Integration Potential

  • Limited compatibility with natural approaches
  • May create window for addressing underlying hormonal issues
  • Requires specific prescribing knowledge
  • Can be part of broader women's health protocols

## 3. REGULATORY AND PRECEDENT CONTEXT

  • Included in various international formularies
  • Not on WHO Essential Medicines List

### Comparable Medications

  • Related to norethindrone
  • Part of combined oral contraceptive class
