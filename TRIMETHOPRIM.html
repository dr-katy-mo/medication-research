2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Trimethoprim functions as a competitive inhibitor of bacterial dihydrofolate reductase, blocking the conversion of dihydrofolate to tetrahydrofolate. This disrupts folate-dependent biosynthetic processes including DNA synthesis, ultimately leading to bacterial cell death. The selectivity arises from the 20,000-fold higher affinity for bacterial versus human dihydrofolate reductase. The medication interfaces with natural folate metabolism but selectively targets the bacterial version of this essential pathway.

Clinical Utility

Primary applications include treatment of uncomplicated urinary tract infections, respiratory tract infections, and certain gastrointestinal infections. Often used in combination with sulfamethoxazole for synergistic effect. Generally well-tolerated with good oral bioavailability. Typically prescribed for short-term use (3-14 days) rather than long-term therapy. Provides effective antimicrobial therapy with relatively low resistance rates for many common pathogens.
