2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Clindamycin functions as a bacteriostatic antibiotic by binding irreversibly to the 50S ribosomal subunit of susceptible bacteria. It specifically targets the peptidyl transferase center, blocking the attachment of amino acids to the growing peptide chain during protein synthesis. This mechanism is particularly effective against gram-positive bacteria and anaerobes. The drug concentrates well in bone, joint fluid, and abscesses, making it valuable for infections in these sites where natural immune access may be limited.

Clinical Utility

Primary therapeutic applications include treatment of serious anaerobic infections, bone and joint infections, dental infections, and certain skin and soft tissue infections caused by susceptible organisms. It serves as an important alternative for patients allergic to penicillin antibiotics. The medication is particularly valuable for treating *Clostridium difficile*-associated infections (though paradoxically it can also predispose to C. diff overgrowth), anaerobic lung infections, and serious staphylococcal infections. Typical treatment courses range from 7-21 days depending on infection severity and location.
