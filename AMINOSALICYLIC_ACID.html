# AMINOSALICYLIC ACID (PAS) Evaluation

## 1. NATURAL DERIVATION ASSESSMENT

### Source Investigation

  • Structurally related to salicylic acid, naturally found in willow bark (Salix species)
  • Represents a synthetic derivative of salicylic acid with an amino group addition
  • Historical connection to natural salicylates used in traditional medicine
  • No direct natural source extraction; synthesized commercially

### Structural Analysis

  • Core salicylate structure mirrors natural compounds found in plants
  • Contains para-aminobenzoic acid (PABA) structural elements
  • Modification includes amino group (-NH2) addition at para position
  • Maintains carboxylic acid and hydroxyl functional groups common in natural products

### Biological Mechanism Evaluation

  • Interferes with bacterial folate synthesis, similar to natural antifolates
  • Competes with para-aminobenzoic acid, a natural bacterial nutrient
  • Works through metabolic pathways present in mycobacteria
  • Minimal interference with human cellular processes

### Natural System Integration

  • Does not significantly disrupt normal human folate metabolism
  • Supports natural immune response against mycobacterial infection
  • Generally well-tolerated by physiological systems
  • Works in conjunction with host immune defenses

## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action

  • Competitive inhibition of bacterial folate synthesis
  • Bacteriostatic action against Mycobacterium tuberculosis
  • Selective toxicity for bacterial cells over human cells
  • May have anti-inflammatory properties similar to other salicylates

### Clinical Utility

  • Primary use: Treatment of drug-resistant tuberculosis
  • Secondary use: Inflammatory bowel disease (off-label)
  • Typical dosing: 4-12g daily in divided doses
  • Duration: Extended treatment courses (months to years)
  • Population: Adults with MDR-TB or XDR-TB

### Integration Potential

  • Can be combined with other anti-TB medications
  • Supports conventional treatment protocols
  • May complement immune-supporting natural therapies
  • Requires careful monitoring and supportive care

## 3. REGULATORY AND PRECEDENT CONTEXT

### Current Status

  • FDA-approved prescription medication
  • Available as oral tablets and granules
  • Classified as a second-line anti-tuberculosis agent
  • No controlled substance scheduling

### Key Findings
