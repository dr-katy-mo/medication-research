2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Amlodipine functions by selectively blocking L-type voltage-dependent calcium channels in vascular smooth muscle and cardiac tissue. This blockade reduces calcium influx during depolarization, leading to vasodilation and reduced peripheral vascular resistance. The mechanism works within the natural calcium signaling pathways that regulate vascular tone. The medication has a long half-life (30-50 hours), allowing once-daily dosing and providing consistent calcium channel modulation.

Clinical Utility

Primary applications include hypertension management and angina treatment. Amlodipine is considered a first-line antihypertensive agent with excellent tolerability. It provides cardiovascular protection through blood pressure reduction and improved coronary perfusion. It is typically used long-term for chronic blood pressure management rather than acute interventions.
