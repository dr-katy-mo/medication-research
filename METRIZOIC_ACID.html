Mechanism of Action

Metrizoic acid functions as an ionic radiographic contrast medium through passive physical properties. The three iodine atoms per molecule create high X-ray attenuation, producing contrast in radiographic imaging. After injection, it distributes rapidly through extracellular fluid spaces and is eliminated unchanged primarily through glomerular filtration. Clinical Utility

Primary therapeutic application is enhancement of radiographic imaging, particularly urography and angiography. It enables visualization of vascular structures, renal function, and anatomical abnormalities that would be invisible on plain radiographs. Safety concerns include hypersensitivity reactions, nephrotoxicity, and cardiovascular effects. Use is strictly temporary and procedural.
