2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Naproxen inhibits cyclooxygenase enzymes (COX-1 and COX-2) through reversible competitive inhibition, reducing the conversion of arachidonic acid to prostaglandins and thromboxanes. This results in decreased inflammation, pain, and fever. The mechanism preserves the natural enzymatic pathways while modulating their activity levels, allowing endogenous anti-inflammatory mechanisms to function more effectively.

Clinical Utility

Primary applications include management of inflammatory conditions such as rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, and acute pain conditions. Naproxen offers anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antipyretic effects with a longer half-life than many other NSAIDs, allowing for less frequent dosing. It has an established safety profile when used appropriately, with contraindications including cardiovascular disease, gastrointestinal ulceration, and renal impairment. Generally intended for short-to-moderate term use rather than indefinite therapy.
