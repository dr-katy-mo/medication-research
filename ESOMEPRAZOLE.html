Esomeprazole irreversibly inhibits the H+/K+-ATPase enzyme system at the secretory surface of gastric parietal cells. This enzyme is the final common pathway for gastric acid secretion, representing a naturally occurring target that is evolutionarily conserved. The medication undergoes acid-catalyzed activation in the acidic canaliculi of parietal cells, where it forms covalent disulfide bonds with cysteine residues of the enzyme.
