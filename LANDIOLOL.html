## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action
Landiolol selectively blocks beta-1 adrenergic receptors in cardiac tissue, reducing heart rate, myocardial contractility, and cardiac output. Unlike non-selective beta-blockers, it has minimal effect on beta-2 receptors, preserving bronchial and vascular smooth muscle function. The medication is rapidly hydrolyzed by red blood cell esterases, resulting in an elimination half-life of approximately 4 minutes, allowing for precise cardiovascular control.

### Clinical Utility
Landiolol is primarily used for perioperative blood pressure and heart rate control, particularly during anesthesia and surgical procedures. It is indicated for managing intraoperative and postoperative tachycardia and hypertension. The ultra-short duration of action makes it particularly valuable in situations requiring precise, titratable cardiovascular control. It has demonstrated efficacy in cardiac surgery, neurosurgery, and other procedures where rapid onset and offset of beta-blockade is desired.

### Integration Potential
The medication's ultra-short duration and selective mechanism make it compatible with naturopathic principles of minimal intervention and rapid reversibility. It can create a therapeutic window during acute situations while allowing for implementation of other therapeutic modalities. The precise control it offers may prevent the need for more aggressive interventions or longer-acting medications with more persistent effects.
