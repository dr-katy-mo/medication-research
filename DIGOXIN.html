2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Digoxin's primary mechanism involves selective inhibition of the Na+/K+-ATPase pump, particularly in cardiac myocytes. This inhibition increases intracellular sodium concentrations, which drives increased intracellular calcium through the sodium-calcium exchanger (NCX). The elevated intracellular calcium enhances myocardial contractility (positive inotropic effect). Additionally, digoxin has vagomimetic effects that slow atrioventricular conduction, making it useful for rate control in atrial fibrillation.

Clinical Utility

Primary therapeutic applications include treatment of heart failure with reduced ejection fraction and rate control in atrial fibrillation. Digoxin is particularly valuable in patients who remain symptomatic despite optimal therapy with ACE inhibitors, beta-blockers, and diuretics. It provides symptomatic improvement without mortality benefit in heart failure. The medication has a narrow therapeutic window requiring careful monitoring of serum levels, renal function, and electrolyte balance. It can be used long-term with appropriate monitoring.
