# Naturopathic Formulary Medication Evaluation Protocol
# Evaluation of PEMOLINE

## 1. NATURAL DERIVATION ASSESSMENT

### Source Investigation
Pemoline is a synthetic stimulant medication with no direct natural source derivation. It is entirely laboratory-synthesized and does not have clear historical or traditional medicine usage.

### Structural Analysis

  • Core structure: Oxazolidine ring system
  • No direct structural similarity to natural compounds
  • Synthetic compound with unique chemical architecture
  • Does not mirror common natural product scaffolds

### Biological Mechanism Evaluation

  • Interacts with dopamine and norepinephrine systems
  • Affects natural neurotransmitter pathways
  • Modulates endogenous central nervous system function
  • Works through existing physiological mechanisms but is not biomimetic

## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action

  • Stimulates central nervous system
  • Increases dopamine and norepinephrine activity
  • Exact mechanism not fully understood
  • May inhibit dopamine reuptake

### Clinical Utility

  • Previously used for ADHD treatment
  • Typical dosing: 37.5-75 mg daily
  • Withdrawn from most markets due to hepatotoxicity concerns
  • Limited therapeutic window

### Integration Potential
Low potential for naturopathic integration due to:

  • Significant safety concerns
  • Purely synthetic nature
  • Availability of safer natural alternatives
  • Risk of severe liver complications

## 3. REGULATORY AND PRECEDENT CONTEXT

### Current Status

  • Withdrawn from US market (2005)
  • Schedule IV controlled substance
  • No longer manufactured or prescribed in most countries
  • Significant regulatory restrictions

### Comparable Medications

  • Other CNS stimulants: methylphenidate, amphetamines
  • Natural alternatives: L-theanine, omega-3 fatty acids
  • Botanical options: Bacopa monnieri, Rhodiola rosea

## 4. EVIDENCE DOCUMENTATION

### Primary Sources Consulted

  • FDA documentation
  • PubMed Central
  • DrugBank
  • Toxicology databases

### Key Findings

  • Multiple reports of severe liver toxicity
  • Limited efficacy data compared to alternatives
  • Poor safety profile
  • Withdrawal from market well-documented
