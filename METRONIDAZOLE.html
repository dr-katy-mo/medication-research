## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action

  • Enters bacterial cells through passive diffusion
  • Reduced by bacterial enzymes to form toxic metabolites
  • Disrupts bacterial DNA synthesis
  • Particularly effective against anaerobic organisms

### Clinical Utility

  • Treatment of anaerobic bacterial infections
  • Parasitic infections (particularly protozoa)
  • Bacterial vaginosis
  • Typical dosing: 500mg 2-3x daily for 7-14 days
  • Used in both adult and pediatric populations

### Integration Potential

  • Can be combined with probiotics (time-separated)
  • Supports removal of pathogenic organisms
  • Compatible with dietary modifications
  • Can be integrated with immune-supporting protocols

## 3. REGULATORY AND REGULATORY CONTEXT

### Current Status

  • FDA-approved prescription medication
  • Available in oral, topical, and IV formulations
  • Generic availability
  • Pregnancy category B

### Comparable Medications

  • Other nitroimidazoles (tinidazole)
  • Natural alternatives: berberine, oregano oil
  • Often used with other antimicrobials
