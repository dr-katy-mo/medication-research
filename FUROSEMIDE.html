2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Furosemide functions by reversibly binding to and inhibiting the NKCC2 co-transporter in the kidney's thick ascending limb. This prevents reabsorption of approximately 20-25% of filtered sodium, leading to increased excretion of sodium, chloride, and water. The mechanism directly impacts natural physiological processes of fluid and electrolyte homeostasis, working through endogenous regulatory systems rather than bypassing them.

Primary therapeutic applications include treatment of edema associated with congestive heart failure, hepatic disease, and renal disease, as well as management of hypertension.
