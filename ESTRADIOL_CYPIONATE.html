Estradiol cypionate functions as a prodrug that releases bioidentical estradiol through enzymatic cleavage of the cypionate ester. The released estradiol binds to nuclear estrogen receptors, forming hormone-receptor complexes that regulate gene transcription. This mechanism is identical to natural estradiol signaling and integrates with endogenous feedback mechanisms through the hypothalamic-pituitary axis. The sustained-release formulation provides physiological hormone levels over extended periods.
