Mechanism of Action

Selinexor selectively and irreversibly binds to XPO1 at cysteine-528, blocking the nuclear export of tumor suppressor proteins, growth regulatory proteins, and oncogenic mRNAs. This leads to nuclear accumulation of tumor suppressor proteins, cell cycle arrest, and apoptosis in cancer cells. The mechanism exploits the fact that cancer cells are typically more dependent on XPO1-mediated nuclear export than normal cells, providing some selectivity for malignant tissues.

Clinical Utility

Selinexor is FDA-approved for treatment of relapsed or refractory multiple myeloma (in combination with dexamethasone) and relapsed or refractory diffuse large B-cell lymphoma.
