# Naturopathic Formulary Medication Evaluation Protocol
# Evaluation of DIETHYLSTILBESTROL (DES)

## 1. NATURAL DERIVATION ASSESSMENT

### Source Investigation

  • Fully synthetic compound developed in 1938
  • Not derived from natural sources
  • Structurally designed to mimic natural estrogen
  • No traditional medicine usage history

### Structural Analysis

  • Synthetic non-steroidal estrogen
  • Contains stilbene core structure
  • Structural similarity to estradiol but not directly derived
  • Contains phenol groups similar to natural estrogens

### Biological Mechanism Evaluation

  • Binds to estrogen receptors (ERα and ERβ)
  • Mimics natural estrogen activity
  • Interferes with natural hormone signaling
  • Disrupts endocrine system homeostasis

### Natural System Integration

  • Does not support natural healing processes
  • Disrupts normal endocrine function
  • Interferes with natural hormone balance
  • Known to cause significant adverse effects on development

## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action

  • Acts as a potent synthetic estrogen
  • Binds to estrogen receptors
  • Alters gene expression
  • Affects reproductive system development

### Clinical Utility

  • Historically used for:

- Preventing miscarriage (now contraindicated)
- Hormone replacement therapy
- Prostate cancer treatment

  • Banned for most uses due to serious adverse effects

### Integration Potential

  • Not recommended for integration into naturopathic practice
  • Known carcinogen
  • Transgenerational adverse effects
  • Conflicts with natural healing principles

## 3. REGULATORY AND PRECEDENT CONTEXT

### Current Status

  • FDA banned for most uses in 1971
  • Classified as known human carcinogen
  • Limited use in advanced prostate cancer
  • Significant restrictions on availability

### Comparable Medications
Natural alternatives:

  • Phytoestrogens
  • Black cohosh
  • Red clover
  • Dong quai

Summary of Findings:
DES is a synthetic estrogen with no natural derivation that, while structurally similar to natural estrogen, has been shown to cause significant adverse effects including cancer and developmental abnormalities. Its mechanism disrupts rather than supports natural biological processes. Given its documented risks and contradiction to naturopathic principles, DES is not appropriate for inclusion in a naturopathic formulary.
