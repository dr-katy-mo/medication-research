Mechanism of Action

Halobetasol propionate functions as a potent synthetic glucocorticoid that binds to cytoplasmic glucocorticoid receptors. Upon binding, the hormone-receptor complex translocates to the nucleus where it regulates transcription of glucocorticoid-responsive genes. This results in decreased synthesis of inflammatory mediators including prostaglandins, leukotrienes, and various cytokines, while increasing production of anti-inflammatory proteins.

Clinical Utility

Halobetasol is primarily indicated for topical treatment of inflammatory and hyperproliferative skin conditions including psoriasis, dermatitis, eczema, and lichen planus.
