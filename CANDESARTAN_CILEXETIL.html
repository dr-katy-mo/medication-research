2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Candesartan cilexetil is hydrolyzed during absorption to form candesartan, which selectively binds to and blocks angiotensin II type 1 (AT1) receptors. This prevents angiotensin II from exerting its vasoconstrictive and aldosterone-stimulating effects. The blockade results in vasodilation, reduced sodium retention, and decreased blood volume - all contributing to lower blood pressure. The mechanism works entirely through modulation of existing physiological pathways rather than introducing novel biochemical processes.
