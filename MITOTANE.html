2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Mitotane functions as an adrenolytic agent, causing selective necrosis of adrenocortical cells through disruption of mitochondrial function and steroid hormone synthesis. The compound accumulates preferentially in adrenal cortical tissue and fatty tissues due to its lipophilic properties. It inhibits multiple steroidogenic enzymes and ultimately leads to atrophy of the adrenal cortex, effectively creating a "medical adrenalectomy."

Clinical Utility

Mitotane is primarily indicated for treatment of inoperable adrenocortical carcinoma and severe Cushing's syndrome when surgical intervention is not feasible or has failed. It serves as palliative therapy for advanced adrenocortical cancer and can provide hormonal control in hyperfunctioning adrenal tumors. The medication requires careful monitoring due to its narrow therapeutic window and potential for significant adverse effects including adrenal insufficiency, neurological toxicity, and gastrointestinal disturbances. Treatment is typically long-term, often requiring months to years of therapy.

Integration Potential

The medication could potentially create therapeutic windows for supportive natural interventions during cancer treatment, but would require extensive practitioner education in oncology and endocrinology.
