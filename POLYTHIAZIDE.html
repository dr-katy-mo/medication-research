2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Polythiazide functions by selectively inhibiting the thiazide-sensitive sodium-chloride cotransporter in the distal convoluted tubule of the nephron. This inhibition results in increased excretion of sodium and chloride, with secondary effects on potassium and magnesium excretion. The medication also has mild carbonic anhydrase inhibitory activity. Long-term antihypertensive effects may involve direct vasodilation through mechanisms that are not fully elucidated but appear to involve calcium channel modulation and vascular smooth muscle effects.

Clinical Utility

Polythiazide is primarily indicated for the management of hypertension and edema associated with congestive heart failure, hepatic cirrhosis, and corticosteroid therapy. It is considered a first-line antihypertensive agent, particularly useful in elderly patients and those with isolated systolic hypertension. The medication has a longer duration of action compared to hydrochlorothiazide, allowing for once-daily dosing. Safety profile includes risks of hypokalemia, hyperuricemia, and glucose intolerance, requiring monitoring during use. It is generally considered appropriate for long-term management when indicated.
