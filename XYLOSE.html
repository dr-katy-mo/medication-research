2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Xylose functions as a diagnostic marker for small bowel absorption capacity. After oral administration, it is absorbed in the proximal small intestine through passive diffusion and active transport mechanisms. The absorbed xylose is partially metabolized and partially excreted unchanged in urine. Measurement of urinary xylose excretion or blood xylose levels provides an assessment of small intestinal absorptive function, as xylose absorption correlates with the functional integrity of the intestinal mucosa.

Clinical Utility

Primary therapeutic application is as a diagnostic agent in the D-xylose absorption test, used to evaluate small bowel malabsorption syndromes including celiac disease, tropical sprue, bacterial overgrowth, and other conditions affecting intestinal mucosa. The test helps differentiate small bowel malabsorption from pancreatic insufficiency. It provides valuable diagnostic information with minimal invasiveness compared to intestinal biopsy procedures. The test is generally well-tolerated with few adverse effects, primarily limited to mild gastrointestinal symptoms in some patients.
