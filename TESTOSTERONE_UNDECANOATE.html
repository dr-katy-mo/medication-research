Mechanism of Action

Following intramuscular injection, testosterone undecanoate is slowly hydrolyzed by tissue esterases to release testosterone and undecanoic acid. The released testosterone binds to cytoplasmic androgen receptors, forming hormone-receptor complexes that translocate to the nucleus and regulate transcription of androgen-responsive genes. This results in increased protein synthesis, enhanced muscle mass and strength, improved bone density, and restoration of normal male sexual characteristics and function.

Clinical Utility

Primary therapeutic applications include treatment of male hypogonadism (primary and secondary), delayed puberty in males, and certain cases of breast cancer in women. The long-acting formulation provides sustained testosterone levels for 10-14 weeks, reducing injection frequency compared to shorter-acting preparations.
