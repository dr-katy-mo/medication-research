# Naturopathic Formulary Medication Evaluation Protocol

For POLYETHYLENE GLYCOL:

## 1. NATURAL DERIVATION ASSESSMENT

### Source Investigation

  • Synthetic polymer derived from ethylene oxide
  • No direct natural source
  • No documented traditional medicine use
  • Industrial chemical synthesis process

### Structural Analysis

  • Linear polymer of ethylene oxide units
  • No direct structural similarity to natural compounds
  • Not related to endogenous human compounds
  • Metabolically inert, passes through system unchanged

### Biological Mechanism Evaluation

  • Works primarily through osmotic effects
  • Does not interact with specific receptors
  • Functions through physical rather than biochemical mechanisms
  • Non-absorbable and non-metabolized

### Natural System Integration

  • Works with natural water balance mechanisms
  • Supports natural bowel evacuation processes
  • Enables natural colonic motility
  • Non-irritating to intestinal mucosa

## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action

  • Osmotic laxative effect
  • Draws water into intestinal lumen
  • Softens stool and increases bulk
  • Facilitates natural peristalsis

### Clinical Utility

  • Treatment of constipation
  • Bowel preparation for procedures
  • Safe for long-term use
  • Well-tolerated with minimal side effects
  • Temporary or maintenance therapy options

### Integration Potential

  • Compatible with dietary and lifestyle interventions
  • Supports transition to natural bowel regulation
  • Minimal practitioner training needed
  • Can be used alongside other natural therapies

## 3. REGULATORY AND PRECEDENT CONTEXT

### Current Status

  • FDA approved as generally recognized as safe (GRAS)
  • Available in various molecular weights
  • Widely accepted in international formularies
  • Included in WHO Essential Medicines List

### Comparable Medications

  • Similar osmotic laxatives in formularies
  • Less harsh than stimulant laxatives
  • Preferred over more aggressive interventions
