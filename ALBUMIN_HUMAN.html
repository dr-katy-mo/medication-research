# Evaluation of ALBUMIN HUMAN

## 1. NATURAL DERIVATION ASSESSMENT

### Source Investigation

  • Direct human-derived protein isolated from blood plasma
  • Historical use in medicine dating back to World War II for treating shock and burns
  • Produced through fractionation of human plasma donations
  • 100% identical to endogenous human albumin

### Structural Analysis

  • Natural human protein consisting of 585 amino acids
  • Single polypeptide chain with 17 disulfide bridges
  • Molecular weight of approximately 66.5 kDa
  • Three homologous domains with specific binding sites for various substances

### Biological Mechanism Evaluation

  • Primary plasma protein responsible for:

- Maintaining oncotic pressure
- Transport of hormones, fatty acids, and drugs
- Antioxidant functions
- pH buffering

  • Interacts with natural receptor systems and transport mechanisms

### Natural System Integration

  • Directly replaces depleted endogenous albumin
  • Supports natural fluid balance mechanisms
  • Enhances natural detoxification through binding capabilities
  • Maintains physiological homeostasis
  • Works within normal protein metabolism pathways

## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action

  • Maintains plasma oncotic pressure
  • Provides volume expansion in circulation
  • Facilitates transport of various molecules
  • Acts as free radical scavenger
  • Binds and transports bilirubin, drugs, and other compounds

### Clinical Utility

  • Treatment of:

- Hypoalbuminemia
- Shock
- Burns
- Hypovolemia
- ARDS
- Surgical blood loss

  • Available in 5% and 25% solutions

### Integration Potential

  • Highly compatible with naturopathic principles as it:

- Replaces natural compound
- Supports physiological functions
- Works with body's natural systems
- Facilitates healing processes

## 3. REGULATORY AND PRECEDENT CONTEXT

### Current Status

  • FDA-approved biological product
  • Prescription only
  • Regulated as a blood product
  • Subject to strict quality control and safety measures

### Comparable Medications

  • Other plasma expanders:

- Synthetic colloids (hydroxyethyl starch)
- Natural alternatives limited
- No direct natural substitutes available
