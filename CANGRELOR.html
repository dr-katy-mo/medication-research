Cangrelor functions as a direct-acting, reversible P2Y12 receptor antagonist. It competitively binds to the P2Y12 receptor on platelets, preventing ADP-induced platelet activation and aggregation. Cangrelor is primarily indicated as an adjunct to percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) to reduce the risk of periprocedural myocardial infarction, repeat coronary revascularization, and stent thrombosis in patients not treated with a P2Y12 inhibitor or glycoprotein IIb/IIIa inhibitor. Its rapid onset and offset make it uniquely suited for situations requiring immediate antiplatelet effects with predictable recovery. The medication has demonstrated superior efficacy compared to clopidogrel in preventing periprocedural ischemic events.
