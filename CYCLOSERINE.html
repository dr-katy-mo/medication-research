Mechanism of Action

Cycloserine functions as a structural analog of D-alanine, competitively inhibiting alanine racemase (which converts L-alanine to D-alanine) and D-alanyl-D-alanine synthetase (which forms the dipeptide precursor for peptidoglycan cross-linking). This dual inhibition disrupts bacterial cell wall synthesis, leading to cell wall instability and bacterial death. The mechanism is bacteriostatic at lower concentrations and bactericidal at higher concentrations, working synergistically with the host immune system.
