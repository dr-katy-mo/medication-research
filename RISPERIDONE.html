Mechanism of Action

Risperidone acts primarily as a dopamine D2 receptor antagonist and serotonin 5-HT2A receptor antagonist. It also has affinity for alpha-1 adrenergic receptors and histamine H1 receptors. The medication modulates neurotransmitter activity in limbic and cortical brain regions, helping to normalize aberrant dopaminergic hyperactivity associated with psychotic symptoms while the serotonin antagonism may contribute to mood stabilization and reduced extrapyramidal side effects.

Clinical Utility

Risperidone is primarily indicated for schizophrenia, bipolar disorder (manic or mixed episodes), and irritability associated with autism spectrum disorder in pediatric patients. It is considered a first-line atypical antipsychotic with a well-established safety profile. The medication can be used both short-term for acute symptom management and long-term for maintenance therapy. It is available in multiple formulations including oral tablets, oral solution, and long-acting injection.
