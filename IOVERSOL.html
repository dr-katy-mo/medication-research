2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Ioversol is a non-ionic, low-osmolar iodinated contrast medium. Its mechanism is purely physical - the iodine atoms absorb X-rays due to their high atomic number, creating radiographic contrast between tissues and organs. After intravenous administration, the compound distributes through the vascular and extracellular spaces, is not metabolized, and is eliminated unchanged primarily through glomerular filtration by the kidneys within 24 hours.

Clinical Utility

Primary applications include contrast enhancement for computed tomography (CT) scans, angiography, and urography. The compound enables visualization of vascular structures, organ perfusion, and pathological processes that would be invisible on non-contrast imaging. It has a favorable safety profile compared to older ionic contrast agents, with lower osmolality reducing adverse reactions. Use is temporary and diagnostic in nature, typically involving single administrations for specific imaging procedures.
