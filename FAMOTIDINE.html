2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Famotidine acts as a competitive antagonist at histamine H2-receptors, primarily located on gastric parietal cells. When histamine binds to these receptors, it normally triggers a cascade involving cyclic adenosine monophosphate (cAMP) that ultimately activates the hydrogen-potassium ATPase pump, leading to acid secretion. Famotidine blocks this natural signaling pathway, reducing both basal and stimulated gastric acid secretion. The medication also reduces pepsin secretion indirectly by creating a less acidic environment. This mechanism works within existing physiological regulatory systems rather than bypassing them.

Clinical Utility

Famotidine is primarily used for treating peptic ulcer disease, gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), Zollinger-Ellison syndrome, and prevention of stress-related gastric bleeding. It is available both by prescription and over-the-counter for heartburn relief. The medication has a favorable safety profile with minimal drug interactions and is generally well-tolerated. Most therapeutic applications involve temporary use to allow healing of acid-related damage, though some conditions may require longer-term management. Famotidine is often preferred over proton pump inhibitors for short-term use due to fewer long-term side effects.
