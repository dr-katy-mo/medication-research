Flutemetamol F-18 crosses the blood-brain barrier and selectively binds to amyloid-beta plaques with high affinity. The fluorine-18 isotope undergoes positron decay with a half-life of approximately 110 minutes. The emitted positrons interact with electrons to produce gamma photons detectable by PET scanners. The compound demonstrates preferential binding to areas of high amyloid plaque density, allowing visualization of amyloid burden in living patients.
