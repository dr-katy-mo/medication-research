Urea C-13 functions as a diagnostic substrate for bacterial urease activity, primarily used in the urea breath test for detecting Helicobacter pylori infection. The mechanism relies on H. pylori's production of urease enzyme, which hydrolyzes the labeled urea into ammonia and ¹³CO₂. The isotopically labeled carbon dioxide is then detected in exhaled breath, providing a non-invasive diagnostic method that utilizes natural enzymatic processes.
