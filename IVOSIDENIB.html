2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Ivosidenib selectively inhibits mutant IDH1 enzymes (R132H, R132C, R132G, R132L, R132S) that produce the oncometabolite 2-hydroxyglutarate. This inhibition reduces 2-HG levels, leading to reversal of histone and DNA hypermethylation, restoration of normal cellular differentiation, and induction of blast cell differentiation in acute myeloid leukemia (AML) and cholangiocarcinoma.

Clinical Utility

Primary applications include treatment of relapsed/refractory AML with IDH1 mutations and previously treated cholangiocarcinoma with IDH1 mutations. The medication is used as targeted therapy for specific genetic mutations rather than broad cytotoxic treatment. Safety profile includes QT prolongation risk and differentiation syndrome. Typically used long-term as maintenance therapy.
