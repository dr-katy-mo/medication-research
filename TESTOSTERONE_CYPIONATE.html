2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Testosterone cypionate functions as a long-acting testosterone replacement therapy. Following intramuscular injection, endogenous esterases cleave the cypionate ester, releasing free testosterone over 1-4 weeks. The released testosterone binds to cytoplasmic androgen receptors, forming hormone-receptor complexes that translocate to the nucleus and regulate gene transcription. This activates the same physiological processes as endogenous testosterone, including anabolic effects on muscle and bone, maintenance of secondary sexual characteristics, and regulation of libido and mood.

Clinical Utility

Primary applications include treatment of male hypogonadism (primary and secondary), delayed puberty in males, and certain cases of female-to-male gender dysphoria. It serves as hormone replacement therapy when endogenous testosterone production is insufficient. The medication offers advantages over daily testosterone preparations through its extended-release profile, improving patient compliance. Safety profile is generally favorable when used appropriately, with monitoring for hematocrit elevation, prostate health, and cardiovascular risk factors. Typically used as long-term therapy in cases of permanent hypogonadism.
