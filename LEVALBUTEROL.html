2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Levalbuterol functions as a selective beta-2 adrenergic receptor agonist, binding to these receptors in bronchial smooth muscle and activating the adenylyl cyclase-cAMP signaling cascade. This leads to smooth muscle relaxation, bronchodilation, and reduced airway resistance. The medication also stabilizes mast cells, reducing histamine release and inflammatory mediator production. By selectively targeting the (R)-enantiomer activity, levalbuterol provides bronchodilation with potentially fewer cardiac side effects compared to racemic albuterol.

Clinical Utility

Primary therapeutic applications include treatment and prevention of bronchospasm in asthma, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), and other reversible obstructive airway diseases. It serves as both a rescue medication for acute symptoms and a preventive treatment before exercise or allergen exposure. The medication demonstrates excellent safety and tolerability, with fewer cardiac side effects than racemic albuterol. It is typically used as needed for acute symptoms, though regular use may be appropriate for some patients with persistent symptoms.

Levalbuterol is FDA-approved and classified as a prescription medication available in both nebulizer solution and metered-dose inhaler formulations.
