Mechanism of Action

Iodipamide meglumine acts as a contrast medium by absorbing X-rays due to its high atomic number iodine atoms. After intravenous administration, it is concentrated and excreted by the liver into the bile ducts, providing radiographic visualization of the biliary system during cholangiography.
