2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Chlorthalidone inhibits the thiazide-sensitive sodium-chloride cotransporter in the distal convoluted tubule, leading to increased excretion of sodium, chloride, and water. This results in reduced plasma volume and decreased peripheral vascular resistance through mechanisms involving the renin-angiotensin system and direct vasodilation. The long half-life (40-60 hours) provides sustained blood pressure reduction.

Clinical Utility

Primary applications include treatment of hypertension and edema associated with heart failure, hepatic cirrhosis, and renal disease. Chlorthalidone is considered a first-line antihypertensive agent with extensive cardiovascular outcome data. It has superior cardiovascular protection compared to hydrochlorothiazide in clinical trials. The medication is generally well-tolerated with predictable effects on electrolytes requiring monitoring.
