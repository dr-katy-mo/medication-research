2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Desloratadine functions as a long-acting, selective peripheral H1 histamine receptor antagonist. It prevents histamine from binding to H1 receptors in tissues throughout the body, thereby blocking the cascade of inflammatory responses that characterize allergic reactions. The medication also demonstrates inverse agonist activity, actively stabilizing the receptor in its inactive state. Unlike first-generation antihistamines, desloratadine has minimal penetration of the blood-brain barrier, allowing it to target peripheral histamine activity while preserving natural central nervous system histamine functions.

Clinical Utility

Primary therapeutic applications include treatment of allergic rhinitis (seasonal and perennial), chronic idiopathic urticaria, and other histamine-mediated allergic conditions. The medication provides 24-hour symptom relief with once-daily dosing and has a favorable safety profile with minimal sedating effects. Desloratadine is generally used for both short-term symptomatic relief and longer-term management of chronic allergic conditions. The safety profile includes minimal drug interactions and low incidence of adverse effects, making it suitable for extended use when clinically indicated.
