2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Tafenoquine functions as a tissue schizonticide and gametocytocide, working against both liver and blood stages of malaria parasites. It generates reactive metabolites that interfere with parasite mitochondrial function and DNA replication. The medication affects heme polymerization within the parasite, leading to accumulation of toxic heme products. This multi-target approach works within natural cellular oxidative and metabolic pathways to selectively eliminate parasites.

Clinical Utility

Tafenoquine is primarily used for malaria prophylaxis and radical cure of Plasmodium vivax malaria, including elimination of dormant liver stages (hypnozoites). It offers the advantage of single-dose treatment for radical cure, improving compliance compared to multi-day regimens. The medication has a long half-life allowing for weekly dosing in prophylaxis. Safety considerations include the need for G6PD deficiency screening due to risk of hemolytic anemia in deficient individuals. It represents a significant advancement in addressing relapsing malaria.
