Mechanism of Action

Lurasidone functions as a multi-receptor antagonist, primarily blocking dopamine D2 receptors (Ki = 1.68 nM) and serotonin 5-HT2A receptors (Ki = 0.47 nM). It also acts as an antagonist at 5-HT7 receptors and as a partial agonist at 5-HT1A receptors. This multi-target approach helps modulate neurotransmitter imbalances associated with schizophrenia and bipolar depression while potentially reducing extrapyramidal side effects common with other antipsychotics.

Clinical Utility

Lurasidone is FDA-approved for the treatment of schizophrenia in adults and adolescents (13-17 years), and for bipolar depression as monotherapy or adjunctive therapy with lithium or valproate. It demonstrates efficacy in reducing positive and negative symptoms of schizophrenia and depressive symptoms in bipolar disorder. The medication has a relatively favorable metabolic profile compared to some other atypical antipsychotics, with lower risk of weight gain and metabolic dysfunction. It is typically used as a long-term maintenance therapy.
