2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Desogestrel functions as a selective progesterone receptor agonist with additional weak androgenic activity. After oral administration, it is rapidly converted to its active metabolite 3-keto-desogestrel. This metabolite binds to progesterone receptors in target tissues including the hypothalamus, pituitary, ovaries, and reproductive tract. The primary mechanism involves suppression of the LH surge, preventing ovulation, while also causing changes in cervical mucus viscosity and endometrial characteristics that reduce fertility potential.

Clinical Utility

The primary therapeutic application is hormonal contraception, available both as a progestin-only pill and in combination with ethinyl estradiol. It provides reliable contraceptive efficacy with a pearl index of less than 1 when used correctly. The medication has a favorable safety profile compared to older progestins, with reduced androgenic side effects. It can be used long-term for contraceptive purposes or short-term when temporary fertility suppression is desired. The progestin-only formulation is particularly suitable for women who cannot use estrogen-containing contraceptives.
