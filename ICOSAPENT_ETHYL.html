# Objective: Comprehensive assessment of ICOSAPENT ETHYL

## 1. NATURAL DERIVATION ASSESSMENT

### Source Investigation

  • Derived from fish oil, specifically the omega-3 fatty acid eicosapentaenoic acid (EPA)
  • Naturally occurring in cold-water fatty fish such as salmon, mackerel, sardines
  • Traditional use of fish oil in various cultures for health benefits
  • Purified and processed from marine sources

### Structural Analysis

  • Highly purified ethyl ester of eicosapentaenoic acid (EPA)
  • Structurally similar to naturally occurring EPA in fish oils
  • Related to endogenous omega-3 fatty acids
  • Metabolizes to EPA in the body

### Biological Mechanism Evaluation

  • Interacts with natural inflammatory pathways
  • Reduces triglyceride synthesis and secretion
  • Enhances fatty acid oxidation
  • Integrates with lipid metabolism pathways

## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action

  • Reduces hepatic VLDL-TG synthesis and secretion
  • Increases fatty acid Œ≤-oxidation

### Clinical Utility

  • Anti-inflammatory effects
  • Well-tolerated safety profile

## 3. REGULATORY AND PRECEDENT CONTEXT
