# Naturopathic Formulary Medication Evaluation Protocol
# Evaluation of ACRISORCIN

## 1. NATURAL DERIVATION ASSESSMENT

### Source Investigation
Acrisorcin is a combination medication containing:

  • Acriflavine (synthetic antimicrobial dye)
  • Salicylic acid (naturally derived from willow bark)
  • Resorcinol (originally isolated from tree resins, now typically synthetic)

The combination represents both natural and synthetic components, with salicylic acid having clear natural origins.

### Structural Analysis

  • Salicylic acid maintains its natural plant-derived structure
  • Resorcinol mirrors compounds found in natural resins
  • Acriflavine is a synthetic structure with no direct natural analog

### Biological Mechanism Evaluation
The components work through multiple mechanisms:

  • Salicylic acid: Natural keratolytic activity
  • Resorcinol: Antiseptic properties similar to natural phenolic compounds
  • Acriflavine: Antimicrobial activity

## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action

  • Keratolytic effects (salicylic acid)
  • Antiseptic properties (resorcinol and acriflavine)
  • Primary use in treating fungal skin infections

### Clinical Utility

  • Topical treatment for fungal infections
  • Historical use in dermatological conditions
  • Limited current clinical application

### Integration Potential
Moderate potential for integration due to:

  • Partial natural component basis
  • Topical application limiting systemic effects
  • Compatibility with other natural skin treatments

Summary of Findings:
Acrisorcin represents a historical combination medication with partial natural derivation through its salicylic acid component. While it has documented historical use, modern applications are limited. Its natural components and topical application make it moderately compatible with naturopathic principles, though more effective natural alternatives may exist.

Integration Recommendation:

Focus should be placed on well-documented natural alternatives or current conventional antifungal medications when necessary.
