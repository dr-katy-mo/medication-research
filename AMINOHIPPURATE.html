2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Aminohippurate functions as a diagnostic agent for measuring effective renal plasma flow (ERPF) and tubular secretory function. At low plasma concentrations, it is almost completely cleared by the kidneys in a single pass through active tubular secretion via organic anion transporters. This mechanism mirrors the natural elimination of endogenous organic anions and metabolic waste products, utilizing the same evolutionary conserved transport systems.

Clinical Utility

Primary use is for diagnostic assessment of renal function, particularly in research settings and specialized nephrology evaluations. It provides quantitative measurement of effective renal plasma flow, which is essential for assessing kidney health and function. The compound has excellent safety profile with minimal systemic effects due to rapid renal clearance. Use is typically single-dose for diagnostic procedures rather than chronic administration.
