Trientine functions as a copper-selective chelating agent, forming stable complexes with cupric ions through coordinate covalent bonds with its four nitrogen atoms. The resulting copper-trientine complexes are water-soluble and readily excreted in urine. Unlike penicillamine, trientine has minimal interaction with other metals at therapeutic doses, making it highly selective for copper removal while preserving essential zinc and iron levels.
