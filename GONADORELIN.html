2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Gonadorelin functions as a direct replacement for endogenous GnRH, binding to GnRH receptors on anterior pituitary gonadotroph cells. Upon binding, it activates phospholipase C signaling pathways, increasing intracellular calcium and stimulating the synthesis and release of LH and FSH. These hormones then act on the gonads to stimulate sex hormone production and gametogenesis, maintaining the natural feedback loops of reproductive endocrinology.

Primary applications include diagnostic testing of pituitary function, treatment of hypogonadotropic hypogonadism, and fertility induction.
