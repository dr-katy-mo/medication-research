2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Ciprofloxacin selectively inhibits bacterial DNA gyrase and topoisomerase IV, which are essential for bacterial DNA supercoiling, replication, and chromosome segregation. This leads to bacterial cell death through disruption of DNA processes. The selectivity arises from structural differences between bacterial and human topoisomerases, allowing for antimicrobial activity with relatively lower toxicity to human cells.

Clinical Utility

Ciprofloxacin is indicated for various bacterial infections including urinary tract infections, respiratory tract infections, skin and soft tissue infections, bone and joint infections, and certain gastrointestinal infections. It provides broad-spectrum coverage against both gram-positive and gram-negative bacteria. The medication is generally reserved for more serious infections or when first-line antibiotics are ineffective, supporting its use as a targeted intervention rather than routine therapy.
