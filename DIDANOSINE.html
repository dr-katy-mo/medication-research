2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Didanosine functions as a nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor (NRTI). After intracellular phosphorylation to dideoxyadenosine triphosphate, it competes with natural nucleotides for incorporation into viral DNA by HIV reverse transcriptase. Once incorporated, it causes DNA chain termination due to lack of a 3'-hydroxyl group. This mechanism exploits the dependency of HIV on reverse transcription while minimally affecting normal human DNA polymerases.

Clinical Utility

Primary therapeutic application is as part of combination antiretroviral therapy for HIV infection. It was historically significant as one of the early HIV medications but is now rarely used due to significant toxicities including pancreatitis, peripheral neuropathy, and lactic acidosis. Modern HIV treatment typically employs safer, more effective alternatives. It requires careful monitoring and has numerous drug interactions and contraindications.
