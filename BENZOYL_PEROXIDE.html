## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action
Benzoyl peroxide functions through oxidative decomposition, releasing nascent oxygen that exerts bactericidal effects against Propionibacterium acnes and other anaerobic bacteria. The compound also demonstrates keratolytic properties, promoting desquamation of comedogenic material. This dual mechanism addresses both bacterial colonization and follicular obstruction in acne pathogenesis.

### Clinical Utility
Primary therapeutic applications include topical treatment of acne vulgaris, with concentrations ranging from 2.5% to 10%. The medication serves as first-line therapy for mild to moderate acne and is often used in combination with other topical agents. It demonstrates superior safety profile compared to oral antibiotics for acne treatment, with minimal systemic absorption. Generally used for extended periods with good tolerability when properly titrated.

### Integration Potential
Benzoyl peroxide shows good compatibility with naturopathic therapeutic modalities, particularly when used to create a therapeutic window for natural interventions. It can reduce bacterial burden while other natural therapies address underlying hormonal or dietary factors. The topical application allows for localized treatment without significant systemic effects, making it suitable for integration with comprehensive treatment plans.

Benzoyl peroxide is FDA-approved as an over-the-counter topical antimicrobial agent under monograph conditions. It has been classified as Category I (safe and effective) for acne treatment by FDA advisory panels. The compound maintains widespread international approval for topical dermatological use.
