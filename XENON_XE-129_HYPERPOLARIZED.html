## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action
Hyperpolarized xenon-129 functions as a magnetic resonance imaging contrast agent by exploiting the enhanced nuclear magnetization achieved through optical pumping. When inhaled, it distributes according to natural ventilation patterns and dissolves in lung tissues and blood based on tissue-specific solubility coefficients. The hyperpolarized state provides dramatically enhanced MRI signal that allows real-time visualization of gas distribution, ventilation dynamics, and tissue perfusion without ionizing radiation or nephrotoxic contrast agents.

### Clinical Utility
The primary therapeutic application is diagnostic imaging of pulmonary ventilation and gas exchange in patients with respiratory disorders. It provides functional information about lung physiology that complements anatomical imaging modalities. The medication offers superior safety compared to gadolinium-based contrast agents, particularly in patients with renal impairment. It enables assessment of lung function in pediatric populations where radiation exposure from CT scans poses significant concerns. The xenon is exhaled within minutes, making this a temporary diagnostic intervention rather than long-term pharmaceutical treatment.

## 3. REGULATORY AND PRECEDENT CONTEXT

### Current Status
Hyperpolarized xenon-129 received FDA approval in 2022 as a diagnostic imaging agent under the trade name XENOVIEW. It is classified as a diagnostic radiopharmaceutical agent despite containing no radioactive isotopes. The approval was based on clinical trials demonstrating safety and efficacy for pulmonary MRI applications. International regulatory agencies including Health Canada and the European Medicines Agency have also evaluated or approved similar hyperpolarized gas formulations.
