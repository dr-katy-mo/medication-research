### Mechanism of Action
Gabapentin enacarbil functions as a prodrug that is converted to gabapentin after absorption. Gabapentin binds to the Œ±2Œ¥ subunit of voltage-gated calcium channels, reducing calcium influx into presynaptic terminals. ### Clinical Utility

The medication offers improved bioavailability compared to immediate-release gabapentin, allowing for more predictable therapeutic effects. It has a favorable safety profile with the most common side effects being somnolence and dizziness. The medication is typically used for long-term management of chronic conditions, though it can provide a therapeutic window during which other interventions may be implemented.
