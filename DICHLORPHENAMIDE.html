2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Dichlorphenamide inhibits carbonic anhydrase enzymes, particularly CA-II and CA-IV isoforms. This inhibition reduces the conversion of carbon dioxide and water to bicarbonate and protons, leading to mild metabolic acidosis and altered electrolyte handling in the kidneys. In periodic paralysis, this mechanism helps stabilize muscle membrane potential and prevents the abnormal shifts in potassium that trigger paralytic episodes.

Clinical Utility

Primary therapeutic application is for primary periodic paralysis (both hypokalemic and hyperkalemic forms). It reduces the frequency and severity of paralytic episodes, significantly improving quality of life for patients with these rare genetic conditions. The medication has a favorable safety profile for long-term use and is generally well-tolerated. It serves as first-line therapy for periodic paralysis prevention.
