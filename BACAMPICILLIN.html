2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Bacampicillin functions as a prodrug that releases ampicillin after oral administration. Intestinal esterases hydrolyze the ester linkages, liberating ampicillin, which then irreversibly binds to bacterial PBPs. This binding prevents the final transpeptidation step in peptidoglycan synthesis, leading to cell wall instability and bacterial lysis. The mechanism specifically targets bacterial cell wall synthesis pathways not present in human cells, providing selective antimicrobial activity while working through naturally evolved recognition systems.

Clinical Utility

Bacampicillin is indicated for mild to moderate bacterial infections, including respiratory tract infections, urinary tract infections, and soft tissue infections caused by susceptible organisms. It offers improved oral bioavailability compared to ampicillin, requiring less frequent dosing. The medication provides broad-spectrum activity against gram-positive and some gram-negative bacteria. Safety profile is similar to other penicillins, with primary concerns being hypersensitivity reactions and gastrointestinal disturbances.
