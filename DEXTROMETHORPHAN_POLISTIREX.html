2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Dextromethorphan functions primarily as a non-competitive NMDA receptor antagonist in the central nervous system, specifically affecting the cough center in the medulla. It also acts as a sigma-1 receptor agonist and affects monoamine reuptake transporters. The antitussive effect results from elevating the threshold for cough reflex activation rather than completely suppressing respiratory function. The polistirex formulation provides extended release over 12 hours through ionic binding to the polymer resin.

Clinical Utility

Primary application is as a cough suppressant for non-productive coughs associated with upper respiratory tract infections. It provides symptomatic relief that can improve sleep and reduce respiratory tract irritation. The extended-release formulation offers longer duration of action compared to immediate-release forms. The medication has an excellent safety profile with minimal respiratory depression, making it suitable for outpatient use. It is intended for temporary use during acute respiratory conditions rather than chronic administration.
