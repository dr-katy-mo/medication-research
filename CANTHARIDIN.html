## 1. NATURAL DERIVATION ASSESSMENT

### Source Investigation

  • Natural source: Derived from blister beetles (Meloidae family), particularly Spanish Fly (Lytta vesicatoria)
  • Historical use in traditional Chinese medicine as "Ban Mao"
  • Traditional extraction through beetle collection and processing
  • Cannot be practically synthesized commercially due to complex structure

### Structural Analysis

  • Bicyclic terpenoid structure
  • Natural defensive compound produced by beetles
  • No direct human endogenous analog
  • Metabolizes to non-toxic products

### Biological Mechanism Evaluation

  • Inhibits protein phosphatase 1 and 2A
  • Causes acantholysis in epidermis
  • Interacts with natural cellular processes
  • Triggers controlled inflammatory response

### Natural System Integration

  • Targets naturally occurring cellular enzymes
  • Creates controlled, localized tissue response
  • Stimulates natural healing mechanisms
  • Used primarily as topical vesicant
  • Works within existing inflammatory pathways
