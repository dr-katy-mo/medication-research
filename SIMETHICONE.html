# Naturopathic Formulary Medication Evaluation Protocol
# SIMETHICONE Evaluation

## 1. NATURAL DERIVATION ASSESSMENT

### Source Investigation
Simethicone is a mixture of polydimethylsiloxane (PDMS) and silicon dioxide. While not directly derived from natural sources, silicon dioxide is naturally occurring in minerals. The compound represents a synthetic modification of naturally occurring silicon-based compounds.

### Structural Analysis

  • Core structure: Silicon-oxygen backbone (siloxane)
  • Methyl group modifications
  • Similar silicon-oxygen bonds found in natural silica compounds
  • Non-toxic, inert polymer structure

### Biological Mechanism Evaluation

  • Physical rather than pharmacological action
  • Works by reducing surface tension of gas bubbles
  • Does not interact with biological receptors or enzyme systems
  • Non-absorbable and passes through GI tract unchanged

### Natural System Integration

  • Supports natural digestive processes
  • Does not interfere with normal GI function
  • Facilitates natural gas elimination
  • Compatible with body's mechanical digestive processes

## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action

  • Acts as an antifoaming agent
  • Reduces surface tension of gas bubbles in digestive tract
  • Causes bubble coalescence, allowing easier passage of gas
  • No chemical interaction with body systems

### Clinical Utility

  • Treatment of gas/bloating
  • Relief of infant colic
  • Carminative effects
  • OTC availability
  • Safe for all age groups
  • Typical dosing: 40-125mg PRN

### Integration Potential

  • Highly compatible with naturopathic treatments
  • Can complement dietary modifications
  • Supports rather than replaces natural processes
  • Useful in combination with probiotics and digestive enzymes
