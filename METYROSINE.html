# Naturopathic Formulary Medication Evaluation Protocol
# METYROSINE Evaluation

## 1. NATURAL DERIVATION ASSESSMENT

### Source Investigation
Metyrosine (α-methyltyrosine) is a synthetic analog of tyrosine, a naturally occurring amino acid. While the medication itself is synthetic, it's directly inspired by and structurally related to this essential amino acid found in protein-rich foods.

### Structural Analysis

  • Core structure based on L-tyrosine
  • Modified with addition of a methyl group at the alpha carbon
  • Maintains the phenol group characteristic of tyrosine
  • Stereochemistry similar to natural L-tyrosine

### Biological Mechanism Evaluation

  • Competitively inhibits tyrosine hydroxylase
  • Interferes with natural catecholamine synthesis pathway
  • Interacts with endogenous enzyme systems involved in neurotransmitter production
  • Mimics natural substrate (tyrosine) in cellular processes

### Natural System Integration

  • Affects natural catecholamine synthesis pathways
  • Modulates endogenous neurotransmitter production
  • Works through existing enzymatic systems
  • May impact natural homeostatic mechanisms related to sympathetic nervous system function

## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action

  • Inhibits tyrosine hydroxylase, the rate-limiting enzyme in catecholamine synthesis
  • Reduces production of norepinephrine, epinephrine, and dopamine
  • Results in decreased catecholamine levels in neural and adrenal tissues

### Clinical Utility

  • Primary use: Treatment of pheochromocytoma
  • Reduces catecholamine-related symptoms
  • Typical dosing: 250mg-4g daily in divided doses
  • Used pre-operatively and in inoperable cases

### Integration Potential

  • Can be integrated with dietary modifications
  • Supports management of sympathetic overactivity
  • May complement stress-reduction techniques
  • Requires careful monitoring of blood pressure and side effects

## 3. REGULATORY AND PRECEDENT CONTEXT

### Current Status

  • FDA-approved prescription medication
  • Brand name: Demser
  • Orphan drug status for pheochromocytoma
  • Schedule: Non-controlled substance

### Comparable Medications

  • Alpha-adrenergic blockers (phenoxybenzamine)
  • Beta-blockers
  • Natural alternatives: Limited due to condition severity
