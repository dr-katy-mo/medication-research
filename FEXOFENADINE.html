It binds to H1 receptors with high affinity, preventing histamine from activating these naturally occurring receptors. This blockade interrupts the histamine-mediated cascade that leads to vasodilation, increased vascular permeability, smooth muscle contraction, and sensory nerve stimulation. Clinical Utility

Fexofenadine is primarily indicated for the treatment of seasonal and perennial allergic rhinitis and chronic idiopathic urticaria. It provides relief from symptoms including sneezing, rhinorrhea, nasal congestion, itching, and hives. The medication offers advantages over first-generation antihistamines due to its non-sedating profile and lack of anticholinergic effects. It has a good safety profile with minimal drug interactions, making it suitable for long-term use in chronic allergic conditions.
