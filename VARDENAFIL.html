2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Vardenafil selectively inhibits phosphodiesterase type 5 (PDE5) with high specificity compared to other phosphodiesterases. PDE5 is the predominant enzyme responsible for degradation of cGMP in penile smooth muscle. During sexual arousal, nitric oxide is released, activating guanylate cyclase and increasing cGMP levels, leading to smooth muscle relaxation and increased blood flow. Vardenafil enhances this natural process by preventing cGMP breakdown, allowing the normal physiological erectile response to occur more effectively.

Clinical Utility

Vardenafil is primarily indicated for erectile dysfunction in adult men. It has demonstrated efficacy across various underlying causes of erectile dysfunction, including diabetes, post-prostatectomy, and age-related causes. The medication has a relatively rapid onset (30-60 minutes) and duration of 4-5 hours, allowing for more natural timing of sexual activity. It is generally well-tolerated with side effects primarily related to its vasodilatory effects (headache, flushing, nasal congestion). Vardenafil is intended for episodic use rather than continuous daily therapy in most cases.
