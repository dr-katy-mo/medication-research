2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Olodaterol selectively binds to and activates beta-2 adrenergic receptors in bronchial smooth muscle cells. This activation triggers a cascade involving G-protein activation, adenylyl cyclase stimulation, increased cAMP production, and subsequent activation of protein kinase A, ultimately resulting in smooth muscle relaxation and bronchodilation. The medication has a duration of action of approximately 24 hours, making it suitable for once-daily dosing.

Clinical Utility

Olodaterol is primarily indicated for the long-term maintenance treatment of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), including chronic bronchitis and emphysema. It is typically administered via inhalation using the Respimat inhaler device. The medication provides sustained bronchodilation and can improve lung function and quality of life in COPD patients. It is generally well-tolerated with side effects similar to other beta-2 agonists, including potential cardiovascular effects at higher doses.
