# Naturopathic Formulary Medication Evaluation Protocol
# METYRAPONE Evaluation

## 1. NATURAL DERIVATION ASSESSMENT

### Source Investigation
Metyrapone is a synthetic compound with no direct natural source derivation. It was developed specifically as a pharmaceutical agent to inhibit steroid synthesis.

### Structural Analysis

  • Core structure: Contains a pyridine ring and ketone functional groups
  • No direct structural similarity to natural compounds, though it interfaces with natural steroid synthesis pathways
  • Synthetic design specifically targets 11β-hydroxylase enzyme

### Biological Mechanism Evaluation

  • Interacts with endogenous steroid synthesis pathway
  • Blocks 11β-hydroxylase enzyme, which is involved in cortisol production
  • Works within natural feedback mechanisms of the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis

### Natural System Integration

  • Modulates rather than suppresses natural endocrine function
  • Works with existing biological feedback systems
  • Temporary and reversible effects on cortisol synthesis
  • Does not permanently alter natural endocrine pathways

## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action

  • Selective inhibition of 11β-hydroxylase
  • Reduces conversion of 11-deoxycortisol to cortisol
  • Increases ACTH production through feedback mechanisms
  • Used diagnostically to assess HPA axis function

### Clinical Utility

  • Primary use: Diagnostic testing for HPA axis function
  • Secondary use: Management of Cushing's syndrome
  • Typical dosing: 750mg-6g daily, divided doses
  • Short-term diagnostic use or monitored therapeutic use

### Integration Potential

  • Can be used alongside adrenal support herbs
  • Complements stress reduction techniques
  • Requires careful monitoring of adrenal function
  • Best used as part of comprehensive endocrine support plan

## 3. REGULATORY AND PRECEDENT CONTEXT

### Current Status

  • FDA-approved prescription medication
  • Available as oral capsules
  • Requires careful medical monitoring
  • Specialized endocrine diagnostic tool

### Comparable Medications

  • Ketoconazole (also affects steroid synthesis)
  • Aminoglutethimide
  • Natural alternatives: adaptogenic herbs, though with different mechanisms
