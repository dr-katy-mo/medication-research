2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Acetaminophen functions primarily through central COX inhibition, reducing prostaglandin E2 synthesis in the brain and spinal cord. Unlike NSAIDs, it has minimal effect on peripheral COX activity, preserving beneficial prostaglandin functions. The drug's metabolite AM404 enhances endocannabinoid signaling by blocking anandamide reuptake, contributing to analgesic effects. It also influences descending serotonergic pain pathways and may interact with TRPV1 channels.

Clinical Utility

Primary applications include mild to moderate pain relief and fever reduction. Acetaminophen is considered first-line therapy for many pain conditions due to its favorable safety profile compared to alternatives. It has minimal anti-inflammatory effects but provides effective analgesia and antipyresis. The medication is generally well-tolerated with appropriate dosing, though hepatotoxicity can occur with overdose. It's typically used for temporary symptom management rather than long-term therapy.
