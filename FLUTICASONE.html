2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Fluticasone functions as a potent glucocorticoid receptor agonist, binding with high affinity to intracellular glucocorticoid receptors. The drug-receptor complex translocates to the nucleus where it acts as a transcription factor, upregulating anti-inflammatory genes (including lipocortin-1) and downregulating pro-inflammatory mediators (including cytokines, chemokines, and adhesion molecules). This mechanism directly parallels the natural anti-inflammatory cascade initiated by endogenous cortisol.

Clinical Utility

Primary applications include allergic rhinitis, asthma, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), and various inflammatory skin conditions. Fluticasone is particularly valued for its high topical potency combined with rapid systemic metabolism, reducing systemic side effects compared to oral corticosteroids. It is typically used for maintenance therapy rather than acute treatment, with safety profiles supporting both short-term and long-term use when appropriately monitored. The medication often prevents the need for systemic corticosteroids or other more invasive interventions.
