# CHENODIOL Assessment

## 1. NATURAL DERIVATION ASSESSMENT

### Source Investigation

  • Natural bile acid found in human bile
  • Endogenous compound synthesized in the liver from cholesterol
  • Historically isolated from bile of various mammals
  • Traditional medicine: Used in Chinese medicine (bear bile)

### Structural Analysis

  • Identical to naturally occurring chenodeoxycholic acid
  • Steroid nucleus structure similar to other natural bile acids
  • Direct relationship to endogenous human bile acids
  • Metabolized to natural bile acid derivatives

### Biological Mechanism Evaluation

  • Acts through natural bile acid pathways
  • Essential role in cholesterol metabolism
  • Supplements natural bile acid pool
  • Integrates with normal enterohepatic circulation

### Natural System Integration

  • Targets natural nuclear receptors (FXR)
  • Maintains bile acid homeostasis
  • Enables natural gallstone dissolution
  • Works within evolutionary conserved bile acid system
  • Prevents need for surgical intervention
  • Facilitates return to normal bile composition

## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action

  • Suppresses hepatic cholesterol synthesis
  • Decreases cholesterol secretion into bile
  • Increases bile acid synthesis
  • Enhances cholesterol solubility in bile

### Clinical Utility

  • Primary use: Gallstone dissolution
  • Alternative to surgical removal
  • Generally well-tolerated
  • Used temporarily (months to years)
  • Requires ongoing monitoring

### Integration Potential

  • Compatible with dietary interventions
  • Supports natural stone dissolution
  • Can be combined with other naturopathic approaches
  • Requires specific prescribing knowledge
