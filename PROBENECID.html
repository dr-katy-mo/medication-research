The medication works by inhibiting these natural transport mechanisms, thereby affecting the renal tubular secretion of organic acids including uric acid and certain medications.

2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Probenecid functions as a competitive inhibitor of organic anion transporters in the proximal tubules of the kidneys. By blocking OAT1 and OAT3, it reduces the renal excretion of uric acid, paradoxically leading to increased uric acid excretion at therapeutic doses through effects on urate reabsorption transporters (URAT1). It also inhibits the tubular secretion of certain medications, prolonging their plasma half-lives.

Clinical Utility

Primary therapeutic applications include treatment of hyperuricemia associated with gout, prophylaxis of gouty arthritis, and as an adjuvant to increase plasma concentrations of certain antibiotics (particularly penicillins). The medication is generally well-tolerated with a favorable safety profile when used appropriately. It requires temporary to long-term use depending on the underlying condition and is considered a second-line uricosuric agent.

Current Status

Probenecid is FDA-approved as a prescription medication for treatment of hyperuricemia and as an adjuvant to antibiotic therapy. It has been in clinical use since the 1950s with an established safety and efficacy profile. The medication is included in various hospital formularies and is available generically.
