Mechanism of Action

Mebendazole functions by selectively binding to β-tubulin in susceptible helminths, preventing tubulin polymerization and microtubule formation. Clinical Utility

Mebendazole is primarily used for treating intestinal helminth infections including pinworm (Enterobius vermicularis), roundworm (Ascaris lumbricoides), hookworm (Ancylostoma duodenale and Necator americanus), and whipworm (Trichuris trichiura). It has excellent safety profile with minimal absorption from the gastrointestinal tract, leading to few systemic side effects. Treatment courses are typically short (1-3 days depending on the parasite), making it suitable for acute intervention rather than long-term therapy. The medication is well-tolerated across age groups including children.
