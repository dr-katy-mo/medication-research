Structural Analysis

Dydrogesterone is a retro-progesterone, meaning it is the 9β,10α-stereoisomer of progesterone. It shares the basic steroid backbone structure with naturally occurring progesterone and other endogenous steroid hormones. The molecule contains the same functional groups as progesterone (ketone groups at positions 3 and 20, and a double bond between carbons 4 and 5) but differs in the spatial arrangement of the A and B rings. This structural similarity allows it to interact with progesterone receptors, though with different binding affinity and selectivity compared to natural progesterone.

2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Dydrogesterone functions as a selective progesterone receptor modulator, binding to nuclear progesterone receptors and activating progesterone-responsive gene transcription. It maintains the secretory endometrium, reduces uterine contractility, and supports early pregnancy when endogenous progesterone is insufficient. Unlike synthetic progestins, it does not exhibit significant androgenic, estrogenic, glucocorticoid, or mineralocorticoid activity, making it highly selective for progesterone pathways.

Clinical Utility

Primary applications include treatment of luteal phase defects, threatened abortion, habitual abortion, irregular menstruation, dysmenorrhea, and hormone replacement therapy. It is used for both temporary support (such as during early pregnancy) and cyclical long-term use (for menstrual disorders). The safety profile is favorable with minimal side effects compared to other synthetic progestins, and it does not suppress ovulation at therapeutic doses.

3. REGULATORY AND PRECEDENT CONTEXT

Current Status

Dydrogesterone is not FDA-approved in the United States but is widely approved and used in over 100 countries including European Union nations, Canada, and Australia. It has been in clinical use for over 50 years with extensive safety data. The European Medicines Agency (EMA) has approved its use for various indications related to progesterone deficiency.

Comparable Medications

Natural progesterone (bioidentical progesterone) is currently included in some naturopathic formularies. Dydrogesterone represents a middle ground between laboratory-produced progestins and bioidentical progesterone, offering selectivity advantages over both.
