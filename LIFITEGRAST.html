## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action
Lifitegrast selectively binds to the CD11a subunit of LFA-1, preventing its interaction with ICAM-1. This blocks T-cell activation and migration to sites of inflammation on the ocular surface. The mechanism modulates endogenous immune regulatory pathways involved in inflammatory responses, specifically targeting the adhesion cascade that leads to chronic inflammation in dry eye disease.

### Clinical Utility
Primary therapeutic application is treatment of signs and symptoms of dry eye disease (keratoconjunctivitis sicca). The medication provides targeted anti-inflammatory action with topical administration, offering advantages over systemic immunosuppression. Safety profile shows primarily local ocular side effects with minimal systemic absorption. It is typically used as long-term maintenance therapy for chronic dry eye conditions.

### Integration Potential
Lifitegrast demonstrates compatibility with naturopathic therapeutic modalities as it addresses inflammation without broad immunosuppression. It can be integrated into comprehensive treatment plans alongside nutritional support, environmental modifications, and other natural interventions for dry eye management. The targeted mechanism creates therapeutic windows for natural healing processes while addressing underlying inflammatory pathology.
