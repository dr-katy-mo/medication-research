2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Tolterodine acts as a competitive antagonist at muscarinic acetylcholine receptors, particularly M2 and M3 subtypes found in bladder smooth muscle and salivary glands. It reduces detrusor muscle contractions and increases bladder capacity by blocking parasympathetic stimulation. The medication undergoes hepatic metabolism to form 5-hydroxymethyl tolterodine, an active metabolite with similar antimuscarinic activity.

Clinical Utility

Primary therapeutic application is for overactive bladder syndrome with symptoms of urgency, frequency, and urge incontinence. It is available in immediate-release and extended-release formulations. Safety profile includes common anticholinergic side effects such as dry mouth, constipation, and dizziness. Generally intended for long-term management of chronic bladder dysfunction when behavioral modifications prove insufficient.
