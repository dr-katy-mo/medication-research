# Naturopathic Formulary Medication Evaluation Protocol
# ALCOHOL (Ethanol/Ethyl Alcohol)

## 1. NATURAL DERIVATION ASSESSMENT

### Source Investigation

  • Natural origin: Produced through fermentation of sugars by yeasts
  • Historical usage: One of humanity's oldest medicines, used in traditional medicine globally
  • Production: Natural fermentation process or synthetic production from ethylene
  • Traditional medicinal applications documented across numerous cultures

### Structural Analysis

  • Simple two-carbon alcohol (C2H5OH)
  • Naturally occurring in ripening fruits and fermented substances
  • Structurally similar to other naturally occurring short-chain alcohols
  • Stereochemistry: Achiral molecule

### Biological Mechanism Evaluation

  • Interacts with GABA receptors, natural neurotransmitter systems
  • Metabolized through natural liver pathways via alcohol dehydrogenase
  • Affects natural membrane fluidity and permeability
  • Influences multiple endogenous neurotransmitter systems

### Natural System Integration

  • Processed through existing detoxification pathways
  • Body has evolved natural metabolic processes for alcohol
  • Impacts natural barrier systems and membrane functions
  • Influences natural homeostatic mechanisms

## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action

  • Primary: GABA receptor modulation
  • Secondary: Effects on multiple neurotransmitter systems
  • Membrane-disrupting properties
  • Antiseptic properties through protein denaturation

### Clinical Utility

  • External antiseptic
  • Solvent for herbal tinctures
  • Vehicle for medication delivery
  • Historical use as anxiolytic/sedative
  • Topical antimicrobial

### Integration Potential

  • Essential component in herbal medicine preparation
  • Vehicle for botanical extract delivery
  • Compatible with many natural treatment modalities
  • Useful in compounding natural medicines

## 3. REGULATORY AND PRECEDENT CONTEXT

### Current Status

  • FDA approved as inactive ingredient
  • Various concentrations available OTC
  • Regulated by multiple agencies (FDA, ATF)
  • Essential in USP formulations

### Comparable Medications

  • Other alcohols (isopropyl, methyl)
  • Natural alternative solvents (glycerin, vinegar)
  • Other preservation methods (drying, salt)

  • USP/NF
