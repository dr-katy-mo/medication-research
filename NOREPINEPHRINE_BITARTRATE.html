2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Norepinephrine functions as a potent α1-adrenergic agonist with moderate β1-adrenergic activity. It causes vasoconstriction through α1-receptor activation, increasing systemic vascular resistance and blood pressure, while β1-receptor stimulation provides positive inotropic effects on the heart. This mechanism directly mimics the natural stress response system, utilizing the same molecular pathways that regulate cardiovascular function during physiological challenges.

Clinical Utility

Primary applications include treatment of acute hypotensive states, septic shock, cardiogenic shock, and severe hypotension during anesthesia or critical illness. It serves as first-line vasopressor therapy when fluid resuscitation alone is insufficient. The medication is typically used short-term in acute care settings to restore hemodynamic stability while underlying conditions are addressed. It has a well-established safety profile when used appropriately with hemodynamic monitoring, though it requires careful titration and continuous supervision.
