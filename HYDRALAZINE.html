2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Hydralazine functions as a direct-acting peripheral vasodilator, primarily affecting arterioles with minimal effect on venous capacitance vessels. It interferes with calcium utilization in vascular smooth muscle, leading to smooth muscle relaxation and vasodilation. The drug also appears to enhance nitric oxide release and may affect DNA methylation. These mechanisms work within natural cardiovascular regulatory systems to reduce peripheral vascular resistance and blood pressure.

Clinical Utility

Primary therapeutic applications include treatment of hypertension, particularly in hypertensive emergencies, and as part of combination therapy for heart failure (especially in African American patients when combined with isosorbide dinitrate). It serves a critical role when rapid blood pressure reduction is needed or when other antihypertensive agents are contraindicated. The medication has a well-established safety profile when used appropriately, though long-term monotherapy may cause lupus-like syndrome in slow acetylators. It is typically used for both acute situations and chronic management.
