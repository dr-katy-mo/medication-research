2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Travoprost functions as a selective prostaglandin F receptor agonist, binding to FP receptors in ocular tissues and activating phospholipase C signaling pathways. This leads to increased matrix metalloproteinase expression and extracellular matrix remodeling in the ciliary muscle bundles, enhancing aqueous humor drainage through the uveoscleral pathway. The medication also increases conventional outflow through Schlemm's canal by affecting trabecular meshwork function. These mechanisms mirror and enhance natural physiological processes for maintaining optimal intraocular pressure.

Clinical Utility

Travoprost is primarily indicated for reducing elevated intraocular pressure in patients with open-angle glaucoma or ocular hypertension. It is typically used as a first-line therapy due to its once-daily dosing, efficacy, and generally favorable safety profile compared to systemic medications or surgical interventions.
