Letermovir inhibits CMV replication by targeting the viral terminase complex, which is essential for viral DNA processing and packaging into capsids. Unlike other CMV antivirals that target the viral DNA polymerase, letermovir's novel mechanism reduces the likelihood of cross-resistance. Letermovir is primarily indicated for prophylaxis of CMV infection and disease in adult CMV-seropositive recipients of allogeneic hematopoietic stem cell transplants. It represents a significant advancement in CMV prevention due to its favorable safety profile compared to traditional agents like ganciclovir or foscarnet. It is intended for temporary prophylactic use during the high-risk period post-transplant, typically for up to 100 days.
