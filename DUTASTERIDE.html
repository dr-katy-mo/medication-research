## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action

  • Dutasteride functions as a dual 5Œ±-reductase inhibitor, blocking both type I and type II isoenzymes
  • Reduces DHT levels by approximately 90% in serum and prostate tissue
  • Works within natural steroid hormone metabolism pathways
  • Maintains testosterone levels while specifically reducing DHT conversion

### Clinical Utility

  • Primary indication for benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH) treatment
  • Secondary use for androgenetic alopecia (male pattern baldness)
  • Provides non-surgical management option for prostate enlargement
  • Generally well-tolerated with side effects related to reduced DHT activity
  • Long-term use typically required for sustained benefit
