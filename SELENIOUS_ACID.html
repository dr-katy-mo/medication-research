## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action
Selenious acid functions as a selenium donor in biological systems, where it is reduced and incorporated into selenocysteine residues of selenoproteins. It supports the activity of glutathione peroxidases, which protect cells from lipid peroxidation and oxidative damage. The compound also supports thioredoxin reductase activity, contributing to cellular redox regulation and protein folding. Additionally, it plays a role in thyroid hormone metabolism through selenodeiodinase enzymes and supports immune system function through various selenium-dependent pathways.

### Clinical Utility
Primary therapeutic applications include selenium deficiency correction, particularly in patients with inadequate dietary intake or increased selenium requirements. It is used in clinical settings for parenteral nutrition supplementation and in cases where selenium deficiency contributes to cardiomyopathy, muscle weakness, or immune dysfunction. The compound has applications in supporting antioxidant status and thyroid function. Safety profile is well-established when used within appropriate dosing ranges, though selenium toxicity can occur with excessive intake. It is typically used for both acute correction of deficiency and maintenance supplementation.

### Integration Potential
Selenious acid is highly compatible with naturopathic therapeutic modalities as it addresses fundamental nutritional deficiency and supports endogenous antioxidant systems.
