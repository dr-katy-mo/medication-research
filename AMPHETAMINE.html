2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Amphetamine functions as a monoamine releasing agent, primarily affecting dopamine and norepinephrine systems. It reverses the direction of monoamine transporters, increases vesicular monoamine release, and inhibits monoamine oxidase activity. These actions result in increased synaptic availability of dopamine and norepinephrine, particularly in brain regions associated with attention and executive function. The mechanism works within existing neurotransmitter regulatory systems.

Clinical Utility

Primary therapeutic applications include attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) and narcolepsy.
