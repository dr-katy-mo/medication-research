2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Butenafine functions as a selective inhibitor of fungal squalene epoxidase (squalene 2,3-epoxidase), blocking the conversion of squalene to squalene epoxide in the ergosterol biosynthesis pathway. This results in depletion of ergosterol and accumulation of squalene within fungal cells, leading to altered membrane permeability and cell death. The selectivity for fungal versus human enzymes allows for targeted antifungal activity with minimal impact on human sterol metabolism.

Clinical Utility

Butenafine is primarily used topically for dermatophyte infections including tinea pedis (athlete's foot), tinea corporis (ringworm), and tinea cruris (jock itch). It demonstrates broad-spectrum activity against common dermatophytes and has fungicidal properties. The medication offers once-daily dosing with relatively short treatment courses (1-4 weeks depending on indication). Safety profile shows minimal systemic absorption with topical use and generally good tolerability. It is considered for temporary use to resolve acute fungal infections.
