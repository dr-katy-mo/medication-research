2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Indapamide selectively inhibits the thiazide-sensitive sodium-chloride cotransporter in the distal convoluted tubule, reducing sodium and chloride reabsorption. This leads to increased excretion of sodium, chloride, and water, resulting in reduced plasma volume and decreased peripheral vascular resistance. The drug also has direct vasodilatory effects independent of its diuretic action, possibly through calcium channel modulation and effects on vascular smooth muscle.

Clinical Utility

Primary therapeutic applications include treatment of essential hypertension and edema associated with heart failure. Indapamide is considered a first-line antihypertensive agent with proven cardiovascular outcome benefits. It has a favorable safety profile compared to traditional thiazide diuretics, with less impact on glucose and lipid metabolism. The medication is typically used for long-term management of hypertension, with demonstrated reduction in cardiovascular morbidity and mortality.
