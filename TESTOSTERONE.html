2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Testosterone functions by binding to cytosolic androgen receptors, forming hormone-receptor complexes that translocate to the nucleus and act as transcription factors to regulate gene expression. This genomic mechanism controls protein synthesis, cellular growth, and differentiation. Non-genomic effects occur through rapid signaling pathways affecting ion channels and second messenger systems. The hormone also serves as a substrate for 5α-reductase (producing DHT) and aromatase (producing estradiol), integrating into broader hormonal networks.

Clinical Utility

Primary therapeutic applications include treatment of primary and secondary hypogonadism, delayed puberty in males, and certain cases of female hypogonadism. Testosterone replacement therapy addresses symptoms including decreased libido, erectile dysfunction, fatigue, decreased muscle mass, bone density loss, and mood changes associated with hormone deficiency. Treatment is typically long-term for hypogonadal conditions, with careful monitoring of serum levels, hematocrit, and prostate health. Safety considerations include potential cardiovascular effects, polycythemia, and prostate enlargement.
