1. NATURAL DERIVATION ASSESSMENT

Source Investigation

LACTIC ACID is derived from natural sources. It is produced by lactic acid bacteria (Lactobacillus species) during fermentation of carbohydrates in dairy products, fermented vegetables, and other foods. It naturally occurs in muscle tissue during anaerobic metabolism, in blood, and is produced by various microorganisms in the human microbiome. Plants also produce lactic acid during certain metabolic processes, particularly under anaerobic conditions. Commercial production utilizes fermentation of corn starch, sugar beet, or other carbohydrate sources using Lactobacillus bacteria, maintaining the natural biosynthetic pathway.

Lactic acid is a simple alpha-hydroxy acid with the molecular formula C₃H₆O₃. It is structurally identical to the lactic acid produced endogenously in human cells during glycolysis under anaerobic conditions. The compound exists in two stereoisomeric forms (L-lactic acid and D-lactic acid), with L-lactic acid being the predominant form in human metabolism. Its structure is fundamentally a carboxylic acid with a hydroxyl group, making it chemically identical to the naturally occurring metabolite.
