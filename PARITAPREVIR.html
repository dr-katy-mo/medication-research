## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action
Paritaprevir functions as a direct-acting antiviral (DAA) that specifically inhibits the HCV NS3/4A serine protease. This enzyme is crucial for processing the HCV polyprotein into functional viral proteins necessary for replication. By binding covalently to the active site serine residue, paritaprevir blocks viral protein processing, effectively halting viral replication and allowing the natural immune system to clear the infection.

### Clinical Utility
Primary therapeutic application is the treatment of chronic hepatitis C genotypes 1 and 4 infections. It is used as part of combination therapy regimens, typically with ritonavir (as a pharmacokinetic enhancer), ombitasvir, and sometimes dasabuvir and ribavirin. The medication offers cure rates exceeding 95% with treatment durations of 8-24 weeks. It represents a significant safety improvement over previous interferon-based therapies, with minimal side effects and contraindications.

### Integration Potential
Paritaprevir demonstrates excellent compatibility with naturopathic therapeutic modalities as it creates a therapeutic window by eliminating viral load, allowing implementation of liver-supportive botanicals, nutritional interventions, and lifestyle modifications. It enables comprehensive treatment plans focused on hepatic restoration and immune system optimization following viral clearance.

## 3. REGULATORY AND PRECEDENT CONTEXT

### Current Status
Paritaprevir received FDA approval in December 2014 as part of combination products (Viekira Pak, Technivie). It is approved by the European Medicines Agency and multiple international regulatory bodies. It is included in WHO treatment guidelines for hepatitis C and is considered a first-line therapy for appropriate genotypes.
