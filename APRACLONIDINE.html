## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action
Apraclonidine selectively activates Œ±2-adrenergic receptors in ocular tissues, leading to decreased aqueous humor production and increased uveoscleral outflow. The mechanism involves reducing adenylyl cyclase activity and cAMP levels in ciliary epithelial cells, which decreases sodium and fluid transport into the aqueous humor. This occurs through the same receptor-mediated pathways that naturally regulate intraocular pressure in response to sympathetic nervous system activity.

### Clinical Utility
Apraclonidine is primarily used for short-term reduction of intraocular pressure in patients with glaucoma or ocular hypertension, particularly after laser surgery procedures. It provides rapid onset of action within one hour and can prevent dangerous pressure spikes following certain eye procedures. The medication is generally well-tolerated with minimal systemic absorption, making it safer than systemically administered alternatives for temporary pressure control.

Apraclonidine is FDA-approved as a prescription ophthalmic solution for reducing intraocular pressure. It was first approved in 1988 and is available in 0.5% and 1% concentrations.
