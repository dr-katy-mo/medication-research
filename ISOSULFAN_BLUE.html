2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Isosulfan blue functions as a lymphatic mapping agent by binding selectively to lymphatic proteins and being rapidly taken up by lymphatic vessels when injected. The blue coloration allows visual identification of sentinel lymph nodes during surgical procedures. This is purely a diagnostic/procedural aid mechanism rather than a therapeutic one.

Clinical Utility

Primary application is in sentinel lymph node biopsy procedures, particularly in breast cancer and melanoma surgeries. It serves as a surgical mapping tool to identify the first lymph node(s) that drain a tumor site. The medication is used only during surgical procedures and is not intended for long-term therapeutic use. Safety concerns include rare but serious allergic reactions and potential for blue discoloration of skin and urine.
