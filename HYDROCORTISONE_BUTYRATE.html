This results in anti-inflammatory, immunosuppressive, and antiproliferative effects through modulation of inflammatory mediators, cytokines, and cellular responses.

2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Hydrocortisone butyrate functions as a topical glucocorticoid by binding to intracellular glucocorticoid receptors and modulating gene expression. It suppresses inflammatory cell migration, reduces capillary permeability, decreases complement activity, and inhibits histamine release. The medication reduces production of inflammatory mediators including prostaglandins, leukotrienes, and cytokines while promoting synthesis of anti-inflammatory proteins like lipocortin-1.

Clinical Utility

Primary applications include treatment of inflammatory dermatoses such as eczema, dermatitis, psoriasis, and allergic skin reactions. It provides medium-potency topical corticosteroid activity with reduced systemic absorption compared to non-esterified formulations. The medication offers favorable safety profile for medium-term use and is suitable for application on face and intertriginous areas where higher-potency steroids are contraindicated.
