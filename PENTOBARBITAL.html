Pentobarbital functions by binding to specific sites on the GABA-A receptor chloride channel complex, enhancing chloride ion influx and causing neuronal hyperpolarization. This results in widespread central nervous system depression affecting the cerebral cortex, brainstem, and spinal cord. The drug reduces neuronal excitability and can progress from sedation to anesthesia to potentially fatal respiratory depression depending on dosage. Unlike natural GABA modulation, pentobarbital's effects are dose-dependent and can override normal homeostatic mechanisms.
