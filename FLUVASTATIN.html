Mechanism of Action

Fluvastatin functions as a competitive inhibitor of HMG-CoA reductase, binding to the enzyme's active site and preventing the conversion of HMG-CoA to mevalonate. This action occurs within the natural cholesterol biosynthetic pathway, disrupting the rate-limiting step and reducing endogenous cholesterol production. Clinical Utility

Fluvastatin is primarily indicated for the treatment of hypercholesterolemia and mixed dyslipidemia, as well as for primary and secondary prevention of cardiovascular events. The medication has a favorable safety profile with relatively low rates of myopathy compared to some other statins, partly due to its unique metabolism through CYP2C9 rather than CYP3A4.
