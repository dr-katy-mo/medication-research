2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Sulopenem etzadroxil is hydrolyzed to its active form, sulopenem, which irreversibly binds to bacterial PBPs, inhibiting the final transpeptidation step of peptidoglycan synthesis. This disrupts bacterial cell wall integrity, resulting in cell lysis and death. The mechanism directly interferes with bacterial reproduction and survival while not affecting human cellular processes, as humans do not synthesize peptidoglycan.
