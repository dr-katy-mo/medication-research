## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action
Ledipasvir is an NS5A inhibitor that binds to the N-terminus of the HCV NS5A protein, preventing its essential functions in viral RNA replication and virion assembly. The NS5A protein is crucial for creating replication complexes and organizing viral particle formation. By blocking this protein, ledipasvir disrupts the viral life cycle without directly interfering with normal human cellular processes.

### Clinical Utility
Ledipasvir is primarily used in combination with sofosbuvir for the treatment of chronic hepatitis C genotypes 1, 4, 5, and 6. It offers a highly effective, well-tolerated oral treatment option with cure rates exceeding 95% in most patient populations. Treatment duration is typically 8-12 weeks, representing a finite intervention that can achieve permanent viral clearance. The medication has a favorable safety profile with minimal drug interactions compared to older interferon-based regimens.
