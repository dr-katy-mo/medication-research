2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Rimantadine specifically inhibits the M2 protein ion channel of influenza A virus, blocking the flow of protons into the viral core. This prevents the acidification necessary for viral RNA release and subsequent replication. The mechanism is highly specific to viral proteins and does not directly interfere with normal human cellular processes.

Clinical Utility

Rimantadine is primarily used for prophylaxis and treatment of influenza A infections. It is most effective when used within 48 hours of symptom onset and can reduce illness duration by 1-2 days. The medication has largely been superseded by neuraminidase inhibitors due to widespread viral resistance. It has a generally favorable safety profile with mild CNS and GI side effects being most common.
