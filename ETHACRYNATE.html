## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action

  • Inhibits sodium and chloride reabsorption in ascending loop of Henle
  • Increases urinary excretion of water, sodium, potassium, and chloride
  • Affects natural electrolyte balance
  • Potent diuretic effect

### Clinical Utility

  • Treatment of edema
  • Management of heart failure
  • Alternative for sulfa-allergic patients
  • Significant side effect profile
  • Generally used short-term

### Integration Potential

  • Limited compatibility with naturopathic principles
  • May be necessary in acute situations
  • Creates significant physiological changes
  • Requires careful monitoring

## 3. REGULATORY AND PRECEDENT CONTEXT
