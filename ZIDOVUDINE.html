2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Zidovudine functions as a thymidine analog that interferes with viral DNA synthesis. After intracellular phosphorylation to zidovudine triphosphate, it competes with natural thymidine triphosphate for incorporation into growing DNA chains synthesized by HIV reverse transcriptase. The incorporation leads to chain termination due to the absence of the 3'-hydroxyl group necessary for phosphodiester bond formation. This mechanism specifically targets viral replication while minimally interfering with normal cellular DNA synthesis.
