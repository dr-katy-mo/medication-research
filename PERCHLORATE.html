2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Perchlorate functions as a competitive inhibitor of the sodium/iodide symporter, effectively blocking iodide uptake into thyroid follicular cells. This results in decreased thyroid hormone synthesis and can be used therapeutically in cases of thyroid storm or severe hyperthyroidism as a rapid-acting anti-thyroid agent. The mechanism directly interferes with normal thyroidal iodide concentration and subsequent hormone production.

Clinical Utility

Perchlorate has limited therapeutic applications, primarily as a second-line treatment for severe hyperthyroidism or thyroid storm when other anti-thyroid medications are contraindicated or ineffective. It provides rapid onset of anti-thyroid effects but carries significant safety concerns including potential for aplastic anemia and other blood dyscrasias. It is used only for short-term treatment due to its toxicity profile. Safer alternatives like methimazole or propylthiouracil are typically preferred.

3. REGULATORY AND PRECEDENT CONTEXT

Current Status

Perchlorate is not approved by the FDA as a medication in the United States due to safety concerns, particularly risk of aplastic anemia. It has been used historically in some countries for hyperthyroidism but has been largely discontinued. The EPA regulates perchlorate as a drinking water contaminant with established reference doses due to its endocrine-disrupting effects. It is not included in WHO Essential Medicines List.

Comparable Medications
