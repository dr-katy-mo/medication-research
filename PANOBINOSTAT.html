Mechanism of Action

Panobinostat inhibits histone deacetylases, leading to increased acetylation of histones and non-histone proteins. This results in altered gene expression, cell cycle arrest, and apoptosis, particularly in malignant cells. The mechanism involves disrupting normal chromatin structure and transcriptional regulation, creating selective cytotoxicity against multiple myeloma cells when combined with other agents.

Clinical Utility

Panobinostat is specifically indicated for treatment of multiple myeloma in patients who have received at least two prior regimens including bortezomib and an immunomodulatory agent. It is used in combination with bortezomib and dexamethasone. The medication has significant toxicity including severe diarrhea, fatigue, nausea, peripheral neuropathy, and cardiac complications. It requires intensive monitoring and is reserved for advanced malignancy treatment.
