2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Florbetaben F-18 binds to beta-amyloid neuritic plaques in the brain with high affinity and selectivity. The fluorine-18 isotope enables positron emission tomography (PET) imaging, allowing visualization of amyloid plaque distribution and density. The tracer crosses the blood-brain barrier, binds to fibrillar amyloid deposits, and provides diagnostic information about amyloid pathology associated with Alzheimer's disease and other neurodegenerative conditions.

Clinical Utility

The primary application is diagnostic PET imaging for detection of beta-amyloid neuritic plaques in adults being evaluated for Alzheimer's disease or other causes of cognitive decline. It is used as a single diagnostic procedure with a very small administered dose. The medication has an excellent safety profile due to minimal systemic exposure and rapid clearance. Use is strictly temporary (single administration) for diagnostic purposes only.
