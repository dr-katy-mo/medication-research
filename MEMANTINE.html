2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Memantine blocks NMDA receptors in a use-dependent, voltage-dependent manner with moderate affinity (Kd ~1-10 μM). Unlike other NMDA antagonists, memantine's rapid off-rate kinetics allow it to block pathologically sustained receptor activation while permitting normal phasic glutamate signaling required for physiological function. This selective blockade helps prevent excitotoxicity associated with chronic neurodegeneration while maintaining normal learning and memory processes. The drug also shows some activity at other receptors including nicotinic acetylcholine receptors and serotonin receptors at higher concentrations.

Clinical Utility

Memantine is FDA-approved for moderate to severe Alzheimer's disease, where it demonstrates modest improvements in cognitive and functional measures. It is often used in combination with cholinesterase inhibitors. The medication has shown efficacy in reducing clinical deterioration and may help with behavioral symptoms of dementia. Safety profile is generally favorable with common side effects including dizziness, headache, and constipation. Long-term use is typical, as the medication aims to slow disease progression rather than provide cure.
