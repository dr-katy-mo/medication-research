2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Ibrutinib functions as a selective, irreversible BTK inhibitor that blocks B-cell receptor signaling. By covalently binding to BTK, it prevents downstream activation of pathways including NF-κB, which are crucial for B-cell survival and proliferation. This mechanism specifically targets the molecular pathways driving B-cell malignancies while preserving other immune functions. The drug's selectivity stems from its specific binding to the unique cysteine residue in BTK's active site.

Clinical Utility

Ibrutinib is indicated for chronic lymphocytic leukemia (CLL), small lymphocytic lymphoma (SLL), mantle cell lymphoma, marginal zone lymphoma, and chronic graft-versus-host disease. It offers an oral alternative to chemotherapy regimens and has shown superior efficacy in certain high-risk patient populations. The medication is generally well-tolerated compared to traditional cytotoxic therapies, with a distinct but manageable side effect profile including bleeding risks and cardiac arrhythmias. Treatment duration varies but often requires long-term administration.
