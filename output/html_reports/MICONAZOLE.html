### Mechanism of Action Miconazole functions as a broad-spectrum antifungal agent by binding to and inhibiting the enzyme 14α-demethylase in fungal cells. By blocking this conversion, miconazole causes accumulation of 14α-methyl sterols and depletion of ergosterol, leading to altered membrane permeability, cellular dysfunction, and ultimately fungal cell death.
