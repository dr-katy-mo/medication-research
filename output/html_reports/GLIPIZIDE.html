### Mechanism of Action Glipizide functions by selectively binding to the sulfonylurea receptor 1 (SUR1) subunit of ATP-sensitive potassium channels in pancreatic beta cells. This binding blocks potassium efflux, causing membrane depolarization and opening of voltage-dependent calcium channels. The resulting calcium influx triggers exocytosis of insulin-containing vesicles. This mechanism directly enhances the natural coupling between glucose sensing and insulin secretion that occurs in healthy pancreatic beta cells.
