2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action Zalcitabine functions as a nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor (NRTI). After intracellular phosphorylation to its active triphosphate form, it competes with natural deoxycytidine triphosphate for incorporation into viral DNA by HIV reverse transcriptase. Clinical Utility

Zalcitabine was primarily used for HIV treatment in combination antiretroviral therapy. Additionally, it was discontinued from the market in 2006 due to significant toxicity concerns, including peripheral neuropathy, pancreatitis, and hepatotoxicity.
