2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action Enzalutamide is a potent androgen receptor signaling inhibitor that works through multiple mechanisms: 1) competitive inhibition of androgen binding to the androgen receptor, 2) inhibition of nuclear translocation of the activated receptor, and 3) inhibition of DNA binding by the androgen receptor. This comprehensive blockade of androgen signaling is approximately 5-8 times more potent than bicalutamide and maintains activity against mutant androgen receptors that confer resistance to first-generation antiandrogens.

Clinical Utility

Enzalutamide is FDA-approved for metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer, non-metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer, and metastatic castration-sensitive prostate cancer. It has demonstrated significant improvements in overall survival, progression-free survival, and quality of life measures in multiple phase III clinical trials. Common adverse effects include fatigue, hot flashes, hypertension, and potential for seizures (though rare). The medication is intended for long-term use as part of ongoing cancer management.
