2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action Lactulose functions as an osmotic laxative and prebiotic through fermentation by colonic bacteria. Upon reaching the colon undigested, resident bacteria metabolize lactulose to organic acids, creating an acidic environment (pH 5.0-5.5) that stimulates peristalsis and draws water into the intestinal lumen through osmotic action. In hepatic encephalopathy, the acidic environment favors conversion of ammonia to ammonium ions, which are poorly absorbed and eliminated in feces. This dual mechanism addresses both constipation and hyperammonemia through natural metabolic processes.

Clinical Utility

Primary applications include treatment of constipation and prevention/treatment of hepatic encephalopathy. Lactulose is particularly valuable for chronic constipation management as it maintains efficacy with long-term use without causing dependency. In hepatic encephalopathy, it serves as first-line therapy for reducing episodes and maintaining remission. The medication has an excellent safety profile with minimal systemic absorption, making it suitable for long-term use when indicated. Side effects are generally mild and dose-dependent, consisting primarily of flatulence, bloating, and diarrhea with excessive dosing.
