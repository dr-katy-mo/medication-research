### Mechanism of Action Acetaminophen functions primarily through central COX inhibition, reducing prostaglandin E2 synthesis in the brain and spinal cord. Unlike NSAIDs, it has minimal effect on peripheral COX activity, preserving beneficial prostaglandin functions. Clinical Utility

Primary applications include mild to moderate pain relief and fever reduction. Acetaminophen is considered first-line therapy for many pain conditions due to its favorable safety profile compared to alternatives.
