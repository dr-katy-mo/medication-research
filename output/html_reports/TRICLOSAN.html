2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action Triclosan regulates bacterial fatty acid synthesis by binding to the active site of enoyl-acyl carrier protein reductase (ENR), specifically the FabI enzyme in bacteria. This prevents the bacteria from synthesizing fatty acids necessary for building cell membranes and other vital functions. At higher concentrations, it acts as a non-specific biocide affecting multiple cellular targets.

Clinical Utility

Triclosan has been used as a topical antimicrobial agent in soaps, toothpastes, and other personal care products. Additionally, its use has become increasingly restricted due to concerns about antimicrobial resistance, endocrine disruption, and environmental persistence. The FDA banned triclosan from consumer hand soaps in 2016 and from consumer antiseptic wash products in 2017. Safety concerns include potential endocrine disruption, development of antimicrobial resistance, and bioaccumulation.
