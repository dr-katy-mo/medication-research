2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action Methamphetamine acts as a potent central nervous system stimulant by increasing the release and blocking the reuptake of dopamine, norepinephrine, and serotonin. It reverses the direction of monoamine transporters, causing them to pump neurotransmitters out of the presynaptic terminal rather than into it. The compound also regulates monoamine oxidase, preventing normal neurotransmitter breakdown. This results in dramatically elevated synaptic concentrations of stimulatory neurotransmitters.

Clinical Utility

In clinical medicine, methamphetamine has very limited therapeutic applications due to its high abuse potential and toxicity profile.
