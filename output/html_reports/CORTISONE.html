It binds to cytoplasmic glucocorticoid receptors, forming hormone-receptor complexes that translocate to the nucleus and bind to glucocorticoid response elements (GREs) on DNA. The hormone maintains blood glucose levels, supports cardiovascular function, and modulates immune responses through evolutionarily conserved mechanisms essential for survival and adaptation to stress.

Primary applications include hormone replacement therapy for primary adrenal insufficiency (Addison's disease), secondary adrenal insufficiency, and congenital adrenal hyperplasia.
