2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action Halothane induces general anesthesia through multiple mechanisms including enhancement of inhibitory neurotransmission via GABA_A and glycine receptors, and depression of excitatory transmission. It also affects voltage-gated ion channels and alters membrane fluidity. The compound produces dose-dependent CNS depression leading to unconsciousness, amnesia, and immobility.

Clinical Utility

Halothane was historically used as an inhalational general anesthetic for surgical procedures. Additionally, its use has been largely discontinued in developed countries due to serious adverse effects including hepatotoxicity (halothane hepatitis), cardiac arrhythmias, and malignant hyperthermia. Safer alternatives like sevoflurane and isoflurane have replaced halothane in modern anesthetic practice. The medication is only used for short-term surgical anesthesia, not long-term treatment.
