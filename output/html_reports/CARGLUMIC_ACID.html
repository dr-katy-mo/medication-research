Source Investigation

CARGLUMIC ACID is identical to compounds naturally produced in the human body. Carglumic acid is a synthetic structural analog of N-acetylglutamate (NAG), an endogenous cofactor essential for carbamoyl phosphate synthetase I (CPS I) activity in the liver. While carglumic acid itself is not directly extracted from natural sources, it mimics the function of N-acetylglutamate, which is naturally produced in human hepatocytes from glutamate and acetyl-CoA by the enzyme N-acetylglutamate synthase.
