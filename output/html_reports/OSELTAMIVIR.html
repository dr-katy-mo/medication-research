2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action Oseltamivir phosphate acts as a prodrug that is rapidly converted to oseltamivir carboxylate by hepatic esterases. The active metabolite selectively regulates influenza A and B virus neuraminidase enzymes by binding to the active site with high affinity. This prevents cleavage of terminal sialic acid residues from viral and cellular glycoproteins, blocking viral release from infected cells and preventing infection of new cells. The mechanism preserves host cell viability while disrupting viral replication cycles. Clinical Utility

Oseltamivir is indicated for treatment and prophylaxis of influenza A and B infections. Prophylactic use is indicated for household contacts of infected individuals and during influenza outbreaks in institutional settings.
