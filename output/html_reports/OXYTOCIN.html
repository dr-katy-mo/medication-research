2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action Oxytocin binds to specific oxytocin receptors in target tissues, particularly uterine smooth muscle and mammary epithelial cells. Receptor binding activates the phospholipase C/inositol phosphate signaling cascade, leading to increased intracellular calcium release from sarcoplasmic reticulum. In uterine muscle, this results in rhythmic contractions. In mammary tissue, it causes contraction of myoepithelial cells surrounding alveoli, leading to milk ejection. The hormone also has central nervous system effects related to social bonding, stress response, and maternal behavior through brain oxytocin receptors.
