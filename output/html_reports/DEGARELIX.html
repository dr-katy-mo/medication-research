2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action Degarelix functions as a selective gonadotropin-releasing hormone (GnRH) receptor antagonist. Unlike GnRH agonists that initially stimulate receptors before causing desensitization, degarelix immediately blocks GnRH receptors in the anterior pituitary, leading to rapid suppression of LH and FSH secretion. This results in immediate reduction of testosterone levels to castrate levels (typically <50 ng/dL or 1.7 nmol/L) within 3 days of administration, without the testosterone surge seen with GnRH agonists.

Clinical Utility

Degarelix is primarily indicated for the treatment of advanced prostate cancer in adult men. It provides androgen deprivation therapy (ADT) as a medical alternative to surgical castration. The medication offers advantages over GnRH agonists by avoiding initial testosterone flare, making it particularly valuable in patients with metastatic disease, spinal cord compression risk, or severe bone pain. It is administered as monthly subcutaneous injections with a specific loading dose protocol followed by maintenance dosing.
