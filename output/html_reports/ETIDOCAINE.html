2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action Etidocaine blocks voltage-gated sodium channels in nerve cell membranes by binding to the intracellular portion of the channel in its open or inactive state. This prevents sodium influx necessary for action potential generation and propagation, resulting in reversible loss of sensation. The blockade is use-dependent, meaning it preferentially affects actively firing neurons. The medication has a long duration of action (4-8 hours) due to its high lipophilicity and protein binding characteristics.

Etidocaine is primarily used for regional anesthesia including epidural, nerve block, and infiltration anesthesia procedures.
