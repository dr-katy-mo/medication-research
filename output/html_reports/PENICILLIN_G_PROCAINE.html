2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action Penicillin G procaine irreversibly binds to bacterial penicillin-binding proteins, particularly PBP1 and PBP3, inhibiting the final transpeptidation step of peptidoglycan synthesis. This results in bacterial cell wall weakness, osmotic lysis, and death. The procaine component provides local anesthetic effect and serves as a depot for slow release, extending the duration of antimicrobial activity. The mechanism is bactericidal against actively dividing organisms and specifically targets bacterial processes absent in human physiology.
