2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action Chlorhexidine exerts its antimicrobial effects through disruption of bacterial cell membrane integrity. The positively charged chlorhexidine molecule is attracted to negatively charged bacterial cell surfaces, leading to membrane disruption, leakage of intracellular components, and ultimately cell death. At low concentrations, it has bacteriostatic effects; at higher concentrations, it is bactericidal. The compound has broad-spectrum activity against gram-positive and gram-negative bacteria, with some activity against fungi and enveloped viruses.

Clinical Utility

Primary therapeutic applications include surgical site preparation, wound irrigation, oral hygiene maintenance, and catheter care. Chlorhexidine is widely used in healthcare settings for infection prevention and control. It has an excellent safety profile for topical use with minimal systemic absorption. The medication is typically used for short-term antimicrobial purposes rather than long-term therapeutic intervention. Common formulations include 2% and 4% solutions for surgical preparation and 0.12% solutions for oral rinse.
