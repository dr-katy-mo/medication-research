Clinical Utility

Troglitazone was primarily indicated for type 2 diabetes mellitus as monotherapy or in combination with insulin. It demonstrated significant efficacy in reducing hemoglobin A1c levels and improving insulin sensitivity. Additionally, the medication was withdrawn from the market in 2000 due to severe hepatotoxicity, including cases of acute liver failure requiring transplantation and some fatalities. The hepatotoxicity appeared to be idiosyncratic and unpredictable, occurring in approximately 1 in 63,000 patients.
