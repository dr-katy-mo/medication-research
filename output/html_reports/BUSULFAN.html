### Mechanism of Action Busulfan is a bifunctional alkylating agent that cross-links DNA strands by forming covalent bonds with nucleophilic sites on DNA bases. Busulfan is primarily used as a myeloablative conditioning agent before hematopoietic stem cell transplantation and in the treatment of chronic myelogenous leukemia.
