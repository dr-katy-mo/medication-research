2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action Mannitol functions primarily as an osmotic agent, creating osmotic gradients across biological membranes due to its poor membrane permeability and limited metabolism. In the kidneys, it increases the osmolality of the glomerular filtrate, reducing water reabsorption and promoting diuresis. In the brain and other tissues, it creates an osmotic gradient between the intravascular and extravascular spaces, drawing water from tissues into the bloodstream. Additionally, mannitol exhibits antioxidant properties by scavenging free radicals, particularly hydroxyl radicals, which may contribute to tissue protection during ischemia-reperfusion events.

Clinical Utility

Primary therapeutic applications include treatment of cerebral edema, reduction of increased intracranial pressure, prevention of acute kidney injury during certain surgical procedures, and promotion of diuresis in cases of fluid overload. It is commonly used in neurosurgery and critical care settings. Mannitol is generally well-tolerated with a established safety profile, though monitoring of fluid and electrolyte balance is important.
