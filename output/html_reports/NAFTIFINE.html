2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action Naftifine functions as a potent inhibitor of squalene epoxidase (squalene monooxygenase), a key enzyme in the ergosterol biosynthesis pathway in fungi. This inhibition leads to accumulation of squalene and depletion of ergosterol in fungal cell membranes, causing membrane instability and fungal cell death. The mechanism is highly selective for fungal cells due to differences in sterol metabolism between fungi and mammals.

Clinical Utility

Naftifine is primarily indicated for topical treatment of dermatophyte infections including tinea pedis (athlete's foot), tinea cruris (jock itch), and tinea corporis (ringworm). It demonstrates broad-spectrum activity against dermatophytes and some yeasts. The medication is well-tolerated with minimal systemic absorption when used topically. It offers effective treatment for superficial fungal infections with once-daily dosing and relatively short treatment courses.
