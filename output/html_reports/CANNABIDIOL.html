Cannabidiol (CBD) is a naturally occurring phytocannabinoid found primarily in Cannabis sativa L. plants, including both marijuana and hemp varieties. It was first isolated from cannabis in 1940 by Adams and colleagues, and its structure was elucidated in 1963 by Mechoulam and Shvo. CBD is produced through biosynthetic pathways in cannabis trichomes, where cannabigerolic acid (CBGA) is converted to cannabidiolic acid (CBDA) via the enzyme CBDA synthase, which then decarboxylates to form CBD. Historical documentation shows cannabis has been used medicinally for over 4,000 years across various cultures, including ancient Chinese, Egyptian, and Ayurvedic medicine systems.
