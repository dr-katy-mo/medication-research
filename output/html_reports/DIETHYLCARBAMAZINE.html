2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action Diethylcarbamazine acts primarily by enhancing host immune responses against filarial parasites. It increases the adherence of microfilariae to phagocytic cells, making them more susceptible to immune destruction. The drug also affects parasite metabolism, particularly arachidonic acid pathways, and may have direct toxic effects on adult worms. It modulates cytokine production and enhances complement activation against parasites.

Clinical Utility

DEC is the primary treatment for lymphatic filariasis caused by Wuchereria bancrofti, Brugia malayi, and Brugia timori. It is also used for tropical pulmonary eosinophilia and loiasis. The medication is typically used in short courses (12-21 days) rather than long-term therapy. It has a well-established safety profile when used appropriately, though it can cause inflammatory reactions due to dying parasites (Mazzotti reaction). There are limited alternatives for filarial infections.
