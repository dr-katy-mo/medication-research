### Mechanism of Action Sulfapyridine regulates bacterial growth by interfering with folate synthesis. It competitively regulates the incorporation of PABA into dihydropteroic acid, a precursor to dihydrofolic acid. This modulates bacterial DNA, RNA, and amino acid synthesis, leading to bacteriostatic effects. Clinical Utility

Historically used for pneumococcal pneumonia and other bacterial infections, sulfapyridine has largely been replaced by more effective antibiotics with better safety profiles. Modern use is extremely limited due to toxicity concerns and availability of safer alternatives.
