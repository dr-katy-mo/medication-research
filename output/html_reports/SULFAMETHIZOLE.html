2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action Sulfamethizole functions by competitive inhibition of para-aminobenzoic acid (PABA) in bacterial folate synthesis. It binds to the enzyme dihydropteroate synthase, preventing the formation of dihydropteroic acid from PABA and pteridine, thereby blocking the synthesis of dihydrofolate and subsequently tetrahydrofolate. This disruption prevents bacterial DNA synthesis and cell division, resulting in bacteriostatic activity. The mechanism selectively targets bacterial metabolism without directly interfering with human folate utilization, as humans obtain folate from dietary sources rather than synthesizing it de novo.

Clinical Utility

Sulfamethizole is primarily indicated for urinary tract infections caused by susceptible gram-positive and gram-negative bacteria, including E. coli, Klebsiella, Enterobacter, and Proteus species. The medication has a relatively favorable safety profile compared to broader-spectrum antibiotics, with common side effects being gastrointestinal upset and rare and serious reactions including blood dyscrasias and skin reactions.
