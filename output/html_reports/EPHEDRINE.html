Ephedrine is a naturally occurring alkaloid found primarily in plants of the genus Ephedra (family Ephedraceae), commonly known as Mormon tea or ma huang. The compound was first isolated from Ephedra sinica in 1885 by Japanese chemist Nagai Nagayoshi. Ephedra species have been used in traditional Chinese medicine for over 5,000 years under the name "ma huang" for treating respiratory conditions, particularly asthma and bronchial congestion. The plant is native to arid regions of Asia, Europe, and the Americas. Commercial ephedrine can be extracted from natural plant sources or produced synthetically, though the synthetic version is molecularly identical to the natural compound.

2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action Ephedrine acts as a mixed-acting sympathomimetic amine with both direct and indirect mechanisms. It directly stimulates α₁, α₂, β₁, and β₂ adrenergic receptors while simultaneously promoting norepinephrine release from sympathetic nerve terminals. This results in bronchodilation, increased cardiac output, vasoconstriction, and central nervous system stimulation. The compound crosses the blood-brain barrier and can enhance alertness and reduce appetite through central adrenergic stimulation. Its effects are less potent than epinephrine and longer-lasting due to slower metabolism by monoamine oxidase and catechol-O-methyltransferase.

Clinical Utility

Primary therapeutic applications include treatment of bronchospasm in asthma, hypotension during anesthesia, nasal congestion, and narcolepsy. Ephedrine has been used as a bronchodilator for over a century and remains valuable in emergency situations where rapid bronchodilation is needed. It serves as an alternative to synthetic β₂ agonists and can be particularly useful when other bronchodilators are unavailable or contraindicated. The medication has a good safety profile when used appropriately, though it requires monitoring for cardiovascular effects. It is typically used for acute, short-term management rather than chronic therapy.

3. REGULATORY AND PRECEDENT CONTEXT

### Current Status Ephedrine is classified as a prescription medication in the United States, regulated under the Combat Methamphetamine Epidemic Act due to its potential use in illegal drug manufacturing. Over-the-counter ephedrine products were banned by the FDA in 2004 following safety concerns related to cardiovascular events, though this was primarily due to misuse in weight-loss supplements rather than appropriate therapeutic use.
