### Mechanism of Action Gramicidin functions by forming transmembrane ion channels in bacterial cell membranes. The linear peptide adopts a β-helix conformation and dimerizes head-to-head to create a channel approximately 4 Å in diameter. This channel selectively conducts monovalent cations while excluding anions and larger molecules. The mechanism is bactericidal and shows activity against gram-positive bacteria, with limited effectiveness against gram-negative bacteria due to the outer membrane barrier.

Gramicidin is primarily used as a topical antibiotic for superficial skin and eye infections. It is commonly formulated in combination with other antibiotics (neomycin, polymyxin B) in ophthalmic preparations and topical ointments. Clinical applications include treatment of bacterial conjunctivitis, superficial eye infections, minor skin wounds, and prevention of infection in minor cuts and abrasions.
