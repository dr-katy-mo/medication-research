### Mechanism of Action Ceftriaxone regulates bacterial cell wall synthesis by binding irreversibly to penicillin-binding proteins (PBPs) located in the bacterial cytoplasmic membrane. This binding prevents the final transpeptidation step of peptidoglycan biosynthesis, leading to cell wall defects and ultimately bacterial cell death. The mechanism exploits a fundamental difference between bacterial and mammalian cells, as human cells lack cell walls and are not affected by this mechanism.
