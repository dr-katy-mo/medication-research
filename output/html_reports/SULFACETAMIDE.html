2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action Sulfacetamide functions as a competitive inhibitor of para-aminobenzoic acid (PABA) in bacterial folate synthesis. It competes with PABA for the active site of dihydropteroate synthase, preventing the formation of dihydropteroic acid, an essential precursor in bacterial folate synthesis. Without adequate folate cofactors, bacteria serves to synthesize purines, thymidine, and certain amino acids necessary for DNA replication and cell division. This results in bacteriostatic activity, stopping bacterial growth rather than directly killing bacteria, which allows the host immune system to clear the infection naturally.
