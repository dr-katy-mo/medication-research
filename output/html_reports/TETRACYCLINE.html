2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action Tetracycline acts as a bacteriostatic antibiotic by reversibly binding to the 30S ribosomal subunit of susceptible bacteria. This binding prevents the attachment of aminoacyl-tRNA to the mRNA-ribosome complex, effectively blocking protein synthesis. The mechanism is highly selective for prokaryotic ribosomes due to structural differences from eukaryotic 80S ribosomes. The compound also exhibits anti-inflammatory properties through inhibition of matrix metalloproteinases and modulation of inflammatory cytokines, independent of its antimicrobial effects.

Clinical Utility

Primary therapeutic applications include treatment of respiratory tract infections, skin and soft tissue infections, sexually transmitted infections (particularly chlamydia and some cases of gonorrhea), Lyme disease, Rocky Mountain spotted fever, and other rickettsial infections. It serves as a first-line treatment for several conditions and as an alternative for patients allergic to penicillin. The medication demonstrates broad-spectrum activity against gram-positive and gram-negative bacteria, as well as atypical organisms. Safety profile includes photosensitivity, gastrointestinal effects, and contraindication in pregnancy and children under 8 due to teeth staining and bone growth effects.
