2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action Piperazine acts as a GABA receptor agonist specifically in parasitic nematodes, causing hyperpolarization of nerve membranes and resulting in flaccid paralysis. This selective action occurs because mammalian and nematode GABA receptors have different structural characteristics, allowing piperazine to preferentially affect parasite nervous systems while having minimal impact on human GABA receptors at therapeutic doses.

Clinical Utility

Primary therapeutic applications include treatment of roundworm (Ascaris lumbricoides) and pinworm (Enterobius vermicularis) infections. Piperazine has been used for decades as a first-line anthelmintic, particularly in pediatric populations due to its relatively favorable safety profile. It is typically used as a short-term treatment (1-3 days) rather than for chronic administration. The medication has largely been superseded by newer anthelmintics like albendazole and mebendazole in many regions.
