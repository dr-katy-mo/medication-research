2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action Ranolazine functions primarily as a selective inhibitor of the late inward sodium current (INa,L) in cardiac myocytes. This late sodium current is normally minimal and becomes enhanced in pathological conditions such as ischemia, heart failure, and certain genetic conditions. By inhibiting this aberrant current, ranolazine reduces intracellular sodium and subsequent calcium overload through the sodium-calcium exchanger. This action helps restore normal cellular energy metabolism and reduces cellular dysfunction that contributes to angina symptoms.

Clinical Utility

Ranolazine is FDA-approved for chronic angina as add-on therapy when symptoms persist with optimal doses of other antianginal medications. It provides symptom relief without significantly affecting heart rate or blood pressure, making it useful for patients who serves to tolerate increases in beta-blockers or calcium channel blockers. The medication has shown efficacy in improving exercise tolerance and reducing angina frequency. It is generally well-tolerated with the most common side effects being dizziness and nausea. The medication is intended for long-term use in chronic stable angina management.
