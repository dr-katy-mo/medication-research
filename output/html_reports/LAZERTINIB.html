2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action Lazertinib functions as an irreversible, covalent inhibitor of EGFR tyrosine kinase. It forms a covalent bond with cysteine residues in the ATP-binding pocket of both wild-type and mutant EGFR proteins, including activating mutations (L858R, exon 19 deletions) and the acquired resistance mutation T790M. This mechanism modulates downstream signaling cascades including PI3K/AKT and RAS/RAF/MEK/ERK pathways that regulate cell proliferation, survival, and apoptosis.

Clinical Utility

Lazertinib is primarily indicated for the treatment of non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) harboring EGFR mutations, particularly T790M resistance mutations that develop after first-line EGFR inhibitor therapy. The medication demonstrates improved central nervous system (CNS) penetration compared to earlier-generation EGFR inhibitors. Clinical trials have shown efficacy in both treatment-naïve and previously treated patients with EGFR-mutant NSCLC.
