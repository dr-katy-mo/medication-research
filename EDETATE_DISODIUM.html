2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Edetate disodium functions as a hexadentate chelating agent, binding metal ions through six coordination sites formed by four carboxyl groups and two amine groups. The compound has highest affinity for trivalent ions (Fe³⁺, Al³⁺) and strong affinity for divalent cations (Pb²⁺, Cu²⁺, Zn²⁺, Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺). Upon metal binding, EDTA forms stable, water-soluble complexes that are readily filtered by glomeruli and excreted unchanged in urine. This process directly supports natural detoxification pathways and metal homeostasis mechanisms.

Clinical Utility

EDTA is FDA-approved for treatment of lead poisoning and hypercalcemia. It serves as a first-line chelation therapy for acute lead toxicity and is used in emergency medicine for severe hypercalcemic crisis. The medication has an established safety profile when used intravenously under medical supervision. Clinical applications focus on acute metal toxicity rather than chronic preventive use. Duration of therapy is typically limited to specific treatment protocols based on metal levels and clinical response.
