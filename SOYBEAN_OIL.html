The oil represents one of the most abundant naturally occurring plant oils, comprising approximately 20% of soybean seed weight.

2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Soybean oil functions as a source of essential fatty acids and calories in clinical nutrition. When administered parenterally, it provides lipid substrates for energy metabolism, cell membrane synthesis, and production of bioactive lipid mediators. The oil undergoes hydrolysis by endogenous lipases, releasing fatty acids that enter normal metabolic pathways. It supports synthesis of phospholipids essential for cellular structure and function, particularly important during periods of increased metabolic demand or malnutrition.

Clinical Utility

Primary therapeutic applications include parenteral nutrition support for patients unable to obtain adequate nutrition enterally, treatment of essential fatty acid deficiency, and provision of concentrated calories in critically ill patients. Soybean oil-based lipid emulsions are considered standard components of total parenteral nutrition protocols. Safety profile is generally excellent when used appropriately, though rare hypersensitivity reactions to soy proteins may occur. Long-term use requires monitoring of triglyceride levels and liver function, with temporary use preferred when possible to allow return to enteral nutrition.
