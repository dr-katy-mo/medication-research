2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

Mechanism of Action

Arsenic trioxide induces differentiation and apoptosis in malignant cells, particularly acute promyelocytic leukemia (APL) cells. It causes degradation of the PML-RAR alpha fusion protein, allowing normal cellular differentiation to resume. The compound generates reactive oxygen species, leading to mitochondrial dysfunction and activation of caspase-dependent apoptotic pathways. It also inhibits angiogenesis and has anti-proliferative effects on various cancer cell types.

Clinical Utility

Primary therapeutic application is for acute promyelocytic leukemia, both as induction and consolidation therapy. It has shown efficacy in relapsed or refractory APL cases. The medication has a well-defined role in specific treatment protocols with established dosing regimens. Safety profile includes risks of differentiation syndrome, QT prolongation, and hepatotoxicity, requiring careful monitoring.
