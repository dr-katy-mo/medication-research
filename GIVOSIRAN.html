## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action
Givosiran is a double-stranded siRNA that targets hepatic ALAS1 mRNA for degradation through the RNAi pathway. Upon cellular uptake, it associates with the RISC complex, leading to sequence-specific cleavage of ALAS1 mRNA, thereby reducing ALAS1 protein synthesis and subsequently decreasing toxic heme precursor accumulation.

### Clinical Utility

  • Primary application: Treatment of acute hepatic porphyria in adults
  • Treatment role: Prophylactic therapy to reduce frequency of acute porphyric attacks
  • Alternatives: Limited alternatives exist; hemin therapy is primarily reactive rather than preventive
  • Safety profile: Generally well-tolerated with injection site reactions being most common adverse effect
  • Use duration: Intended for chronic monthly administration

### Integration Potential

  • Naturopathic compatibility: Works through natural cellular mechanisms without disrupting normal physiological processes
  • Treatment plans: Could complement nutritional and lifestyle interventions for porphyria management
  • Therapeutic window: May provide stability allowing implementation of other supportive therapies
  • Education needs: Requires understanding of RNAi mechanisms and porphyrin metabolism
