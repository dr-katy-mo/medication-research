# Naturopathic Formulary Medication Evaluation Protocol

For CLAVULANATE:

## 1. NATURAL DERIVATION ASSESSMENT

### Source Investigation

  • Originally isolated from Streptomyces clavuligerus in 1976
  • Natural product derived from microbial fermentation
  • No documented traditional medicine use
  • Commercial production via controlled fermentation processes

### Structural Analysis

  • β-lactam structure similar to naturally occurring penicillins
  • Contains unique oxazolidine ring system
  • Acts as a β-lactamase inhibitor
  • Metabolic products include inactive compounds readily cleared by the body

### Biological Mechanism Evaluation

  • Targets bacterial β-lactamase enzymes
  • Protects co-administered β-lactam antibiotics
  • Does not directly interact with human enzymes
  • Works through competitive inhibition of bacterial defense mechanisms

### Natural System Integration

  • Targets bacterial resistance mechanisms
  • Preserves effectiveness of natural and semi-synthetic antibiotics
  • Does not interfere with human enzyme systems
  • Allows for lower doses of primary antibiotics

## 2. THERAPEUTIC CONTEXT

### Mechanism of Action

  • Irreversibly binds to bacterial β-lactamase enzymes
  • Prevents breakdown of companion antibiotics
  • Primarily used in combination with amoxicillin
  • No direct antimicrobial activity

### Clinical Utility

  • Treatment of resistant bacterial infections
  • Usually combined with amoxicillin (Augmentin)
  • Good safety profile when properly prescribed
  • Short-term use for acute conditions

### Integration Potential

  • Compatible with other natural antimicrobial approaches
  • Allows for targeted, time-limited intervention
  • Requires prescription authority
  • Supports comprehensive infection management

## 3. REGULATORY AND PRECEDENT CONTEXT

  • Listed on WHO Essential Medicines List
  • Widely accepted globally
