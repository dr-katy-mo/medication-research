While technetium-99m is a synthetic radioisotope produced in nuclear reactors through molybdenum-99 decay, the succimer component (DMSA) has significant natural connections. DMSA is structurally related to naturally occurring sulfur-containing compounds and amino acids found in biological systems. The compound contains two sulfhydryl groups that mimic the chelating properties of naturally occurring cysteine and other sulfur-containing biomolecules. There is no documentation of traditional medicine use for this specific radiopharmaceutical complex, as it is a modern nuclear medicine preparation.

The chelating properties of DMSA mirror those found in naturally occurring metal-binding proteins and peptides such as metallothioneins and glutathione. The technetium-99m component, while synthetic, integrates into biological systems through its complexation with the naturally-derived chelating framework.
